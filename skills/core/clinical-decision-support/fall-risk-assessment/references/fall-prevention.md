# Fall Prevention Interventions and Medication Reference

## Intervention Protocols by Risk Level

### Low Risk (Morse 0-24, Hendrich II 0-4, TUG < 12 sec)

| Intervention | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Patient education | Fall prevention handout, importance of reporting near-misses |
| Safe footwear | Non-slip soles, closed-toe, properly fitted |
| Room orientation | Demonstrate call light, bathroom location, bed controls |
| Bed position | Lowest position, wheels locked |
| Adequate lighting | Night light in bathroom pathway |
| Personal items | Within reach (glasses, phone, call light, water) |
| Annual screening | Reassess at next visit or if status changes |

### Moderate Risk (Morse 25-44, TUG 12-20 sec)

All low-risk interventions PLUS:

| Intervention | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Fall risk identification | Wristband, room sign, chart/EHR flag |
| Toileting schedule | Every 2 hours while awake, offer bedside commode if distant bathroom |
| Supervised ambulation | Assist with first ambulation after admission or procedure |
| PT evaluation | Balance and gait assessment, exercise program |
| Medication review | Review for high-risk medications (see medication table below) |
| Orthostatic precautions | Rise slowly (sit at edge of bed 1-2 minutes before standing) |
| Hourly rounding | Check on patient needs proactively |

### High Risk (Morse >= 45, Hendrich II >= 5, TUG > 20 sec)

All moderate-risk interventions PLUS:

| Intervention | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Bed/chair alarm | Alert when patient attempts to get up unassisted |
| 1:1 sitter | Consider for patients with confusion/impulsivity and score >75 |
| Low bed position | Consider floor-level bed or mattress on floor |
| Floor mat | Padded mat beside bed to reduce injury if fall occurs |
| Hip protectors | For patients with osteoporosis or prior hip fracture |
| PT/OT urgent consult | Within 24 hours of admission |
| Pharmacy consult | Targeted deprescribing review |
| Vitamin D | 800-1000 IU daily (evidence for fall reduction in deficient patients) |
| Home safety assessment | Before discharge (OT referral) |
| Fall alarm device | Wearable alert for post-discharge (community dwelling) |
| Family education | Include family/caregivers in prevention plan |
| Post-discharge follow-up | Fall risk reassessment within 30 days |

## Medication Classes That Increase Fall Risk

### Highest Risk (Strong Evidence)

| Class | Examples (Generic) | RxNorm (Common) | Mechanism |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Benzodiazepines | diazepam, lorazepam, alprazolam, clonazepam, temazepam, midazolam | 3322, 6470, 596, 2598, 10355, 68091 | Sedation, impaired balance, cognitive slowing, muscle relaxation |
| Non-benzodiazepine hypnotics (Z-drugs) | zolpidem, zaleplon, eszopiclone | 39993, 37418, 283871 | Sedation, next-day drowsiness, complex sleep behaviors |
| Opioids | oxycodone, hydrocodone, morphine, fentanyl, tramadol, codeine | 7804, 5489, 7052, 4337, 10689, 2670 | Sedation, dizziness, cognitive impairment, constipation (straining) |
| First-generation antipsychotics | haloperidol, chlorpromazine | 5093, 2403 | Sedation, extrapyramidal symptoms, orthostatic hypotension |
| Second-generation antipsychotics | quetiapine, risperidone, olanzapine | 51272, 35636, 61381 | Sedation, orthostatic hypotension, metabolic effects |
| Tricyclic antidepressants | amitriptyline, nortriptyline, doxepin | 704, 7486, 3638 | Orthostatic hypotension, sedation, anticholinergic effects |

### High Risk (Moderate Evidence)

| Class | Examples (Generic) | Mechanism |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|
| SSRIs | sertraline, citalopram, escitalopram, fluoxetine, paroxetine | Hyponatremia (SIADH), dizziness, gait instability |
| SNRIs | venlafaxine, duloxetine, desvenlafaxine | Dizziness, orthostatic hypotension, hyponatremia |
| Anticonvulsants | gabapentin, pregabalin, phenytoin, carbamazepine, valproate, topiramate, lamotrigine | Dizziness, ataxia, somnolence, cognitive impairment |
| Alpha-1 blockers | doxazosin, prazosin, terazosin, tamsulosin | Orthostatic hypotension (especially first dose), dizziness |
| Centrally-acting antihypertensives | clonidine, methyldopa | Sedation, orthostatic hypotension, rebound hypertension |
| Skeletal muscle relaxants | cyclobenzaprine, methocarbamol, baclofen, tizanidine, carisoprodol | Sedation, dizziness, weakness |
| Anticholinergics | diphenhydramine, hydroxyzine, oxybutynin, tolterodine, promethazine, scopolamine | Confusion, blurred vision, dizziness, urinary retention (then urgency) |
| First-generation antihistamines | diphenhydramine, chlorpheniramine, meclizine | Sedation, anticholinergic effects |

### Moderate Risk (Contributing Evidence)

| Class | Examples (Generic) | Mechanism |
|-------|-------------------|-----------|
| Loop diuretics | furosemide, bumetanide, torsemide | Orthostatic hypotension, electrolyte depletion (hypokalemia, hyponatremia), dehydration |
| Thiazide diuretics | hydrochlorothiazide, chlorthalidone | Hyponatremia, orthostatic hypotension, dehydration |
| Beta-blockers | metoprolol, atenolol, propranolol, carvedilol | Bradycardia, orthostatic hypotension, fatigue, dizziness |
| ACE inhibitors / ARBs | lisinopril, losartan, valsartan | Orthostatic hypotension, dizziness (especially in dehydration) |
| Nitrates | nitroglycerin, isosorbide | Orthostatic hypotension, syncope |
| Diabetes medications (hypoglycemia risk) | insulin, sulfonylureas (glipizide, glyburide, glimepiride) | Hypoglycemia causing dizziness, confusion, syncope, falls |
| Trazodone | trazodone | Sedation, orthostatic hypotension (commonly used as sleep aid) |
| Mirtazapine | mirtazapine | Sedation, weight gain, orthostatic hypotension |

### Polypharmacy Risk

| Total Medications | Fall Risk Increase |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| 1-4 | Baseline |
| 5-9 | 1.5x increased risk |
| >= 10 | 2-3x increased risk |
| >= 2 CNS-active medications | 2.5x increased risk (regardless of total count) |

## Environmental Modification Checklist

### Hospital/Facility

| Area | Modification | Priority |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Bed area | Bed at lowest height, wheels locked, side rails per protocol | High |
| Flooring | Non-slip, dry, no obstacles, cords secured | High |
| Bathroom | Grab bars, raised toilet seat, non-slip mat, call light in bathroom | High |
| Lighting | Adequate illumination, night lights, accessible light switch | High |
| Personal items | Call light, glasses, phone, water, urinal within reach | High |
| Footwear | Non-slip socks or closed-toe shoes provided | High |
| Clutter | Clear pathways, remove unnecessary equipment | Medium |
| Signage | Fall risk sign on door, colored wristband | Medium |

### Home (Pre-Discharge Assessment by OT)

| Area | Modification | Priority |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Stairs | Handrails on both sides, well-lit, non-slip treads, consider stair lift | High |
| Bathroom | Grab bars at toilet and tub/shower, shower chair, non-slip mat, raised toilet seat | High |
| Bedroom | Night light, phone accessible, bed height appropriate for transfers | High |
| Living areas | Remove throw rugs or secure with double-sided tape, clear pathways 36+ inches wide | High |
| Kitchen | Commonly used items at waist height, step stool with handrail if needed | Medium |
| Outdoors | Repair uneven walkways, adequate outdoor lighting, handrails for porch steps | Medium |
| General | Remove clutter, secure electrical cords, pet awareness, adequate lighting in all areas | High |

## Post-Fall Assessment Protocol

### Immediate Assessment (Within Minutes)

1. **Do not move** the patient until assessment complete (unless in immediate danger)
2. **Vital signs**: BP, HR, RR, SpO2, temperature
3. **Level of consciousness**: GCS, orientation
4. **Pain assessment**: location, severity, character
5. **Injury assessment**: head/face, extremities, spine, skin integrity
6. **Neurological**: pupil response, extremity movement, speech, facial symmetry
7. **Head injury check**: if head struck or loss of consciousness, initiate head injury protocol

### Secondary Assessment

| Assessment | Action | When |
|-----------|--------|------|
| Orthostatic vitals | Supine, sitting, standing BP/HR | When medically stable |
| Blood glucose | Finger stick | Immediately if diabetic or on insulin/sulfonylurea |
| Head CT | If head struck, on anticoagulants, altered mental status, or focal neurological signs | Within 1 hour |
| Extremity X-ray | If pain, deformity, inability to bear weight | Within 2 hours |
| ECG | If syncope suspected | Within 30 minutes |
| Labs | CBC, BMP, INR (if on anticoagulation) | Within 1 hour |

### Documentation Requirements

- Date, time, and location of fall
- Witnessed or unwitnessed
- Activity at time of fall (ambulating, transferring, toileting, reaching)
- Injuries sustained
- Interventions performed
- Notification (provider, family, supervisor)
- Fall risk reassessment (recalculate Morse/Hendrich)
- Updated care plan

### Post-Fall Interventions

1. Reassess and recalculate fall risk scores
2. Update care plan with new interventions
3. Increase fall prevention measures (upgrade risk level)
4. Review and modify medications if contributing
5. Place on fall huddle list for multidisciplinary review
6. Root cause analysis for contributing factors
7. Patient/family education and reassurance
