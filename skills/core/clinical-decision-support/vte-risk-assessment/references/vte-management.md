# VTE Management and Prophylaxis Reference

## D-dimer Decision Rules

### When to Use D-dimer

| Clinical Scenario | D-dimer Role |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Wells DVT <= 1 (unlikely) | If negative: DVT excluded. If positive: compression ultrasound. |
| Wells PE <= 4 (unlikely) | If negative: PE excluded. If positive: CTPA. |
| Geneva <= 5 (unlikely) | If negative: PE excluded. If positive: CTPA. |
| Wells DVT >= 2 (likely) | D-dimer NOT useful -- proceed to imaging regardless |
| Wells PE > 4 (likely) | D-dimer NOT useful -- proceed to imaging regardless |

### D-dimer Thresholds

| Population | Threshold (FEU) | Threshold (DDU) | Notes |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| Standard | < 500 ng/mL | < 250 ng/mL | Most commonly used |
| Age-adjusted (age > 50) | Age x 10 ng/mL FEU | Age x 5 ng/mL DDU | Improves specificity in elderly without reducing sensitivity |

Example: 72-year-old patient. Standard cutoff: 500 ng/mL. Age-adjusted cutoff: 720 ng/mL FEU.

### Conditions That Elevate D-dimer (Reduce Specificity)
- Active cancer
- Pregnancy
- Recent surgery (<4 weeks)
- Active infection / sepsis
- Liver disease
- Renal insufficiency
- Trauma
- DIC
- Hospitalization
- Advanced age

## Imaging Decision Pathways

### Suspected DVT

```
Wells DVT Score
  |
  |-- <= 1 (unlikely) --> D-dimer
  |     |-- Negative --> DVT excluded
  |     |-- Positive --> Compression ultrasound (CUS)
  |           |-- Positive --> DVT confirmed, treat
  |           |-- Negative --> DVT excluded (consider repeat CUS in 5-7 days if high suspicion)
  |
  |-- >= 2 (likely) --> Compression ultrasound (CUS)
        |-- Positive --> DVT confirmed, treat
        |-- Negative --> D-dimer
              |-- Negative --> DVT excluded
              |-- Positive --> Repeat CUS in 5-7 days
                    |-- Positive --> DVT confirmed
                    |-- Negative --> DVT excluded
```

### Suspected PE

```
Wells PE Score
  |
  |-- <= 4 (unlikely) --> PERC rule (if low suspicion)
  |     |-- All PERC met --> PE excluded (no further testing)
  |     |-- PERC not met --> D-dimer
  |           |-- Negative --> PE excluded
  |           |-- Positive --> CTPA
  |                 |-- Positive --> PE confirmed, treat
  |                 |-- Negative --> PE excluded
  |
  |-- > 4 (likely) --> CTPA directly
        |-- Positive --> PE confirmed, treat
        |-- Negative --> Consider lower extremity CUS if clinical suspicion remains high
```

### Alternative Imaging
- **V/Q scan**: If CTPA contraindicated (contrast allergy, renal insufficiency, pregnancy). Low/intermediate/high probability interpretation.
- **MRI**: Limited role. Consider for DVT in pregnancy if ultrasound equivocal.
- **CT venography**: May be added to CTPA for simultaneous DVT assessment.

## VTE Prophylaxis Recommendations by Risk Level

### Based on Caprini Score (Surgical Patients)

| Caprini Score | Risk Level | Prophylaxis |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| 0 | Lowest | Early ambulation only |
| 1-2 | Low | Intermittent pneumatic compression (IPC) devices |
| 3-4 | Moderate | LMWH or UFH + IPC |
| >= 5 | High | LMWH or UFH + IPC. Extended prophylaxis for high-risk surgery. |

### Pharmacologic Prophylaxis Options

| Agent | Dose (Prophylactic) | Renal Adjustment | Duration |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|----------|
| Enoxaparin (LMWH) | 40 mg SQ daily | 30 mg SQ daily if CrCl <30 | Until ambulatory or discharge (7-10 days standard) |
| Heparin (UFH) | 5,000 units SQ q8h or q12h | No adjustment | Until ambulatory or discharge |
| Fondaparinux | 2.5 mg SQ daily | Avoid if CrCl <30 | Until ambulatory or discharge |
| Rivaroxaban (post-THA/TKA) | 10 mg PO daily | Avoid if CrCl <30 | THA: 35 days. TKA: 12 days. |
| Apixaban (post-THA/TKA) | 2.5 mg PO BID | No adjustment needed | THA: 35 days. TKA: 12 days. |

### Extended Prophylaxis Duration

| Surgery Type | Standard Duration | Extended Duration |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Total hip arthroplasty | 10-14 days | 35 days (recommended) |
| Total knee arthroplasty | 10-14 days | 12-14 days minimum |
| Hip fracture surgery | 10-14 days | 35 days (recommended) |
| Major abdominal/pelvic cancer surgery | 7-10 days | 28 days (recommended for cancer patients) |
| General surgery | 7-10 days | Until fully ambulatory |

### Medical Patient Prophylaxis

| Risk Assessment | Prophylaxis |
|----------------|-------------|
| Acutely ill, reduced mobility, VTE risk factors | LMWH 40mg SQ daily or UFH 5000 units SQ q8h |
| Actively bleeding or high bleed risk | IPC devices only |
| Critically ill (ICU) | LMWH preferred. If contraindicated: IPC. |
| Stroke (ischemic, immobile) | LMWH or UFH (start 24-48h after onset if no hemorrhagic transformation) |

## Anticoagulation for Confirmed VTE

### DOAC vs Warfarin Selection

| Factor | DOACs (Preferred) | Warfarin |
|--------|-------------------|---------|
| First-line | Yes (ACCP 2021, ASH 2020) | Second-line |
| Onset | Rapid (no bridging needed for most) | Requires 5-7 days bridging with LMWH |
| Monitoring | No routine monitoring | INR monitoring, target 2-3 |
| Renal impairment (CrCl <30) | Limited options (apixaban may be used cautiously) | Preferred |
| Cancer-associated VTE | LMWH or DOAC (edoxaban, rivaroxaban) | Third-line |
| Antiphospholipid syndrome | Avoid DOACs (inferior to warfarin) | Preferred, INR 2-3 |
| Mechanical heart valve | Contraindicated | Required |
| Pregnancy | Contraindicated | LMWH preferred (not warfarin in 1st trimester) |
| Obesity (>120 kg or BMI >40) | Limited data. Consider anti-Xa monitoring if using. | May be preferred |
| Drug interactions (strong CYP3A4/P-gp) | Significant interactions | Different interaction profile |

### DOAC Dosing for VTE Treatment

| Agent | Initial Phase | Maintenance | Duration Considerations |
|-------|--------------|-------------|------------------------|
| Rivaroxaban | 15mg BID x 21 days | 20mg daily with food | No LMWH lead-in needed |
| Apixaban | 10mg BID x 7 days | 5mg BID | No LMWH lead-in needed |
| Edoxaban | LMWH x 5 days first | 60mg daily (30mg if CrCl 15-50, weight <=60kg) | Requires LMWH lead-in |
| Dabigatran | LMWH x 5 days first | 150mg BID | Requires LMWH lead-in |

### Contraindications to Anticoagulation

**Absolute:**
- Active major bleeding
- Severe uncontrolled hypertension (SBP >200 mmHg)
- Active hemorrhagic stroke
- Major surgery/trauma within 24-72 hours (timing varies)
- Severe thrombocytopenia (platelets <25,000)
- Known HIT (for heparin products only)

**Relative:**
- Platelets 25,000-50,000
- Recent GI bleed (<4 weeks)
- Intracranial neoplasm
- Hepatic failure with coagulopathy
- Active peptic ulcer disease
- Recent lumbar puncture / epidural (<24h)

If anticoagulation contraindicated: consider IVC filter for acute proximal DVT or PE. Remove filter when anticoagulation can be safely initiated.

## Duration of Therapy Guidelines

| Scenario | Duration | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| First provoked VTE (transient risk factor: surgery, trauma, immobilization, OCP) | 3 months | Discontinue after 3 months |
| First unprovoked VTE | 3-6 months minimum, then reassess for extended therapy | Consider indefinite if low bleeding risk |
| Recurrent unprovoked VTE | Indefinite | Strong recommendation for indefinite therapy |
| Cancer-associated VTE | Indefinite (while cancer active or on treatment) | Reassess periodically. LMWH or DOAC preferred. |
| VTE with thrombophilia (antiphospholipid, severe thrombophilia) | Indefinite | Especially if unprovoked |
| Subsegmental PE (incidental, no DVT, low risk) | Surveillance vs 3 months | Shared decision-making |

### Extended Therapy (After Initial 3-6 Months)

| Agent | Dose for Extended Phase | Notes |
|-------|------------------------|-------|
| Apixaban | 2.5 mg BID (reduced dose) | AMPLIFY-EXT: 80% RRR in recurrence vs placebo, no increase in major bleeding |
| Rivaroxaban | 10 mg daily (reduced dose) | EINSTEIN-CHOICE: 70% RRR in recurrence |
| Aspirin | 100 mg daily | Alternative if anticoagulation not tolerated. Less effective than DOACs. |

### Reassessment Factors for Indefinite Therapy

**Favoring continuation:**
- Male sex (higher recurrence risk)
- Elevated D-dimer after stopping (>500 ng/mL at 1 month)
- Residual DVT on ultrasound
- Persistent risk factor (obesity, immobility)
- Post-thrombotic syndrome

**Favoring discontinuation:**
- Female with estrogen-provoked VTE
- High bleeding risk (HAS-BLED >=3)
- Patient preference after shared decision-making
- Isolated distal DVT
