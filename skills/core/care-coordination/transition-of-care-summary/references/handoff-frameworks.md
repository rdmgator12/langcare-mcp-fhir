# Clinical Handoff Communication Frameworks

## I-PASS Handoff Framework

### Overview
I-PASS is an evidence-based handoff bundle developed by the I-PASS Study Group. Implementation has been associated with a 23% reduction in medical errors (Starmer et al., NEJM 2014). It is the recommended framework for structured clinical handoffs.

### Structure

#### I -- Illness Severity

| Classification | Definition | Action |
|----------------|-----------|--------|
| Stable | Patient is stable with no acute issues anticipated | Routine monitoring |
| Watcher | Patient may require increased monitoring or intervention | Close observation, lower threshold for escalation |
| Unstable | Patient is critically ill or actively decompensating | Immediate assessment capability required |

**What to include:**
- One-line illness severity statement
- Current acuity level
- Whether patient is on any continuous monitoring (telemetry, 1:1, etc.)

#### P -- Patient Summary

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Identifying info | Name, age, sex, room/bed, MRN |
| Admission reason | Why they came in |
| Hospital course | Key events, procedures, significant findings (2-3 sentences) |
| Ongoing plan | Current treatment trajectory |
| Current status | How the patient is doing right now |

**What to include:**
- Brief narrative (not a full H&P recap)
- Focus on events that inform current management
- Highlight any changes from prior handoff

#### A -- Action List

| Priority | Timeframe | Examples |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Urgent | Within hours | Pending critical results, time-sensitive medications, procedures scheduled |
| Soon | Within shift | Labs to be drawn, imaging to be completed, consult responses expected |
| Routine | Within 24 hours | Diet advancement, PT/OT evaluation, social work follow-up |

**What to include:**
- Specific tasks with responsible party
- Expected timing
- What to do with results when they come back
- Pending consultations and expected recommendations

#### S -- Situation Awareness and Contingency Planning

| Component | Format |
|-----------|--------|
| Anticipatory guidance | "If [event], then [action]" |
| Known risks | Conditions that could worsen |
| Escalation triggers | Specific vital sign thresholds or symptom changes |

**What to include:**
- Top 1-3 things that could go wrong
- Specific parameter thresholds for escalation (e.g., "If SBP < 90, give 500mL NS bolus and call")
- Code status and any limitations on interventions
- What attending/specialist to contact and when

#### S -- Synthesis by Receiver

| Action | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Receiver summarizes | Confirms understanding |
| Clarifying questions | Fills information gaps |
| Read-back of action items | Verifies task list |
| Agreement on plan | Both parties aligned |

**The receiver should:**
- Restate illness severity assessment
- Confirm action items and their priority
- Ask specific questions about anything unclear
- Confirm who to contact if issues arise

## SBAR Communication Framework

### Overview
SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation) is a standardized communication tool originally developed by the US Navy for nuclear submarine operations. Widely adopted in healthcare for urgent clinical communications and escalation.

### Structure

#### S -- Situation
- State who you are and where you are calling from
- State the patient's name and the reason for the communication
- Describe the immediate concern

**Template:**
"I am [name], [role] on [unit]. I am calling about [patient name] in room [number]. The situation is [describe the immediate concern]."

#### B -- Background
- Relevant diagnosis and admission date
- Pertinent medical history
- Current treatment plan
- Recent changes in condition
- Relevant vital signs or lab results

**Template:**
"The patient was admitted on [date] for [diagnosis]. Relevant history includes [conditions]. Current treatment is [medications/interventions]. Over the past [timeframe], the patient has [describe change]."

#### A -- Assessment
- Your clinical assessment of the situation
- What you think is happening
- How severe is the problem

**Template:**
"I believe the problem is [assessment]. The patient appears [stable/unstable/deteriorating]. I am concerned about [specific concern]."

#### R -- Recommendation
- What you think should happen
- What specific action you are requesting
- Any time sensitivity

**Template:**
"I recommend [specific action]. I need you to [specific request]. Is there anything else you would like me to do in the meantime?"

### When to Use SBAR vs I-PASS
| Scenario | Recommended Framework |
|----------|----------------------|
| Shift-to-shift handoff | I-PASS |
| Calling a physician about a concern | SBAR |
| Transfer to another unit | I-PASS |
| Rapid response / code | SBAR |
| Nurse-to-nurse bedside handoff | BPOC + I-PASS |
| ED to inpatient handoff | I-PASS |
| Discharge to post-acute facility | I-PASS |

## BPOC (Bedside Patient-Oriented Care) Handoff

### Overview
Bedside handoff involves the patient in the transfer of care between nurses. Promotes patient engagement, reduces miscommunication, and satisfies Joint Commission requirements for patient involvement.

### Structure

#### Pre-Bedside (At the Nursing Station)
1. Review chart together (off-going and on-coming nurse)
2. Discuss sensitive information not appropriate for bedside (psychiatric history, abuse concerns, family dynamics)
3. Review I-PASS elements: illness severity, patient summary, action list
4. Review medications due within next 2 hours

#### At the Bedside
1. **Introduce**: Off-going nurse introduces on-coming nurse to patient
2. **Patient verification**: Confirm identity (name and DOB)
3. **Situation**: Brief summary of why they are here and what happened today
4. **Medications**: Review current IV infusions, drip rates, last PRN medications
5. **Lines and devices**: Verify all lines, tubes, drains, and devices
6. **Safety check**: Verify:
   - Fall risk interventions in place (bed alarm, non-skid socks)
   - Call bell within reach
   - Allergy band on
   - Correct ID band
   - DVT prophylaxis in place
   - Skin assessment (pressure injury risk areas)
7. **Patient questions**: Ask patient if they have any questions or concerns
8. **Environment**: Verify room safety, equipment functioning

#### Post-Bedside
1. Complete documentation of handoff
2. Address any concerns raised by patient
3. Verify pending tasks with on-coming nurse

## Handoff Anti-Patterns (What Not to Do)

| Anti-Pattern | Problem | Solution |
|-------------|---------|----------|
| Hallway handoff | Information lost, no documentation | Use structured location and format |
| Data dump | Too much information, key items buried | Use I-PASS structure, prioritize |
| Interruption-heavy handoff | Breaks in communication flow | Designate protected handoff time |
| One-way communication | No verification of understanding | Require receiver synthesis |
| Chart-only handoff | Missing clinical nuance, no questions asked | Always include verbal component |
| Assumption of knowledge | Receiver may not know unit-specific protocols | Be explicit about expectations |
| Omission of contingency plans | Receiver unprepared for deterioration | Always include "if...then..." |

## Handoff Documentation Requirements

### Minimum Documentation Elements

| Element | Required | Joint Commission | CMS |
|---------|----------|-----------------|-----|
| Date and time of handoff | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Sender identification | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Receiver identification | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Patient identification | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Diagnosis/problem list | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Medication list | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Allergies | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Code status | Yes | Yes | Recommended |
| Pending results/tasks | Yes | Yes | Recommended |
| Contingency plans | Yes | Yes | No |
| Receiver acknowledgment | Yes | Yes | No |

### Documentation Tips
- Use standardized templates in the EHR
- Time-stamp all handoff documentation
- Include both structured data and free-text narrative
- Document any discrepancies identified during handoff
- Document patient/family questions raised during bedside handoff
- Store handoff notes where the care team can access them (not just in nursing notes)
