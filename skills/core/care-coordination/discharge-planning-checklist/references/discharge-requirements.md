# Discharge Planning Requirements

## CMS Conditions of Participation (42 CFR 482.43)

### Required Discharge Planning Elements

Hospitals must have an operative discharge planning process that applies to all inpatients and outpatients receiving observation services.

#### 1. Identification of Patients Needing Discharge Planning Evaluation
- All patients must be screened within 24 hours of admission
- High-risk patients requiring formal evaluation:
  - Age 65 or older
  - Multiple chronic conditions
  - Functional limitations (ADL dependencies)
  - Cognitive impairment
  - Inadequate social support
  - History of repeated hospitalizations
  - Psychiatric comorbidities
  - Substance use disorders

#### 2. Discharge Planning Evaluation Must Include
- Assessment of patient's likelihood of needing post-hospital services
- Assessment of availability of appropriate services
- Assessment of patient's capacity for self-care
- Identification of likely post-discharge care needs:
  - Home health services
  - Skilled nursing facility
  - Inpatient rehabilitation
  - Long-term acute care
  - Hospice
  - Outpatient services
  - DME/supplies

#### 3. Discharge Plan Must Address
- Diagnosis and treatment summary
- Medication reconciliation (complete list with changes)
- Follow-up appointments (scheduled, not just recommended)
- Patient education and teach-back verification
- DME and supply orders
- Home health or post-acute referrals
- Emergency contact information
- Return precautions (when to seek emergency care)
- Activity and dietary restrictions
- Wound care or other ongoing treatment instructions

#### 4. Patient/Caregiver Involvement
- Patient and/or caregiver must be involved in discharge planning
- Patient preferences must be considered (choice of post-acute provider)
- Documentation of patient/caregiver understanding (teach-back)
- Written discharge instructions provided in patient's preferred language

#### 5. Timeliness Requirements
- Discharge evaluation must be completed on timely basis
- Discharge plan must be arranged prior to discharge
- Post-acute services must be confirmed, not just requested
- Follow-up appointments must be scheduled with confirmed date/time

## IDEAL Discharge Planning Framework (AHRQ)

### I - Include the Patient and Family
- Assess patient's understanding of their condition
- Identify primary caregiver and their availability
- Determine health literacy level
- Address cultural and language barriers
- Involve patient in creating the discharge plan

### D - Discuss with the Patient
Key discussion topics:
1. **Diagnosis and hospital course**: What happened, what was done, what was found
2. **Medications**: What changed and why, how to take each medication, side effects
3. **Activity**: What they can and cannot do, when to resume normal activities
4. **Diet**: Any restrictions, fluid limits, special requirements
5. **Follow-up**: When, where, and with whom; what to bring to appointments
6. **Warning signs**: Specific symptoms requiring immediate medical attention

### E - Educate the Patient
- Use plain language (6th grade reading level)
- Provide written materials in preferred language
- Use teach-back method: "Can you explain back to me what we discussed?"
- Cover medication management including:
  - Purpose of each medication
  - How and when to take it
  - Common side effects
  - What to do if a dose is missed
  - Medications that were stopped and why

### A - Assess How Well Clinicians Communicate
- Standardized discharge communication tools
- Structured handoff between inpatient and outpatient teams
- Medication reconciliation verified by pharmacist
- Discharge summary sent to PCP within 24 hours

### L - Listen to and Honor Patient Preferences
- Post-acute care provider choice
- Home vs facility preference
- Cultural considerations for care delivery
- Religious or spiritual needs
- Advance directive review and update

## Teach-Back Verification Checklist

### Core Teach-Back Items

For each item, ask the patient to explain in their own words. Document response quality as: Demonstrates understanding / Partial understanding / Does not understand / Unable to assess.

#### 1. Condition Understanding
- [ ] "Can you tell me why you were in the hospital?"
- [ ] "What is the main health problem we are treating?"

#### 2. Medication Understanding
- [ ] "Can you tell me the names of your new medications?"
- [ ] "How will you take [medication name]? When and how much?"
- [ ] "What medications did we stop, and do you understand why?"
- [ ] "What side effects should you watch for?"

#### 3. Activity and Diet
- [ ] "What activities should you avoid for now?"
- [ ] "Are there any changes to what you eat or drink?"

#### 4. Follow-up Care
- [ ] "When is your next doctor's appointment?"
- [ ] "Do you know how to get to the appointment?"
- [ ] "What should you bring to your follow-up visit?"

#### 5. Warning Signs
- [ ] "What symptoms would make you call the doctor right away?"
- [ ] "What symptoms would make you come back to the emergency room?"

#### 6. Self-Care Tasks
- [ ] "Can you show me how you will [wound care / blood sugar check / etc.]?"
- [ ] "Do you have the supplies you need at home?"

#### 7. Support System
- [ ] "Who will help you at home?"
- [ ] "Do you have transportation to your follow-up appointments?"
- [ ] "Do you have a way to get your medications?"

### Documentation Requirements
- Record teach-back results in clinical notes
- If patient does not demonstrate understanding, re-educate and reassess
- Document interpreter use if applicable
- Note caregiver involvement in teach-back
- Record any barriers to understanding (cognitive, language, literacy)

## High-Risk Discharge Criteria

Patients meeting any of the following require enhanced discharge planning:

### Clinical Risk Factors
- LACE index score >= 10
- 30-day readmission within past 12 months
- Heart failure (SNOMED 42343007)
- COPD exacerbation (SNOMED 195951007)
- Pneumonia (SNOMED 233604007)
- Acute MI (SNOMED 22298006)
- Surgical procedure during admission
- ICU stay during admission
- Length of stay > 5 days

### Social Risk Factors
- Lives alone with limited support
- Homelessness or housing instability
- No reliable transportation
- Uninsured or underinsured
- Limited English proficiency
- Low health literacy
- Substance use disorder
- Active psychiatric condition
- History of AMA discharge

### Functional Risk Factors
- New functional limitation (mobility, ADLs)
- Requires new DME
- Requires new home health services
- Cognitive decline or delirium during admission
- Fall during hospitalization
- Unable to perform teach-back

### Enhanced Discharge Planning for High-Risk Patients
1. Multidisciplinary discharge planning conference
2. Post-discharge phone call within 48 hours
3. Transitional care management visit within 7 days
4. Pharmacy consultation for medication management
5. Social work involvement for resource coordination
6. Consider transitional care nurse or health coach
