| Modality | Standard Interval | After Normal Result | After Abnormal Result |
|----------|-------------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Colonoscopy | 10 years | Next in 10 years | Follow-up per findings (see below) |
| FIT | Annual | Repeat in 1 year | Colonoscopy needed |
| mt-sDNA (Cologuard) | 3 years | Repeat in 3 years | Colonoscopy needed |
| Flexible sigmoidoscopy | 5 years | Repeat in 5 years | Full colonoscopy needed |
| CT colonography | 5 years | Repeat in 5 years | Colonoscopy if polyp >= 6mm |

## Risk-Based Screening Triggers

### Conditions That Lower Screening Start Age or Increase Frequency

| Risk Factor | Affected Screening | Modification |
|-------------|-------------------|--------------|
| First-degree relative with CRC < 60 | Colorectal | Start at 40 or 10 years before youngest case; colonoscopy q5y |
| Two+ first-degree relatives with CRC any age | Colorectal | Start at 40; colonoscopy q5y |
| Lynch syndrome (confirmed) | Colorectal | Start at 20-25; colonoscopy q1-2y |
| BRCA1/2 carrier | Breast | Start at 25-30; annual MRI + mammography |
| First-degree relative with breast cancer | Breast | Start 10 years before relative's age at diagnosis (no earlier than 25) |
| Li-Fraumeni syndrome | Multiple | Comprehensive screening per genetic counselor |
| Personal history of chest radiation (age 10-30) | Breast | Start 8 years after radiation; annual MRI + mammography |
| HIV positive | Cervical | Pap at diagnosis, repeat at 6 and 12 months, then annual if normal |
| Immunosuppressed | Cervical | Annual Pap |
| Obesity (BMI >= 30) | Diabetes | Start at 35 (or earlier); every 3 years |
| Gestational diabetes history | Diabetes | Screen every 3 years for life starting at 12 weeks postpartum |
| PCOS | Diabetes | Screen regardless of age/BMI |
| Family history of diabetes | Diabetes | Screen at 35 regardless of BMI |
| 20+ pack-year smoking history | Lung | LDCT annually, age 50-80 |
| Parental history of AAA | AAA | Consider screening even if never smoked |
| Postmenopausal + FRAX >= 9.3% | Osteoporosis | DEXA even if age < 65 |
| Long-term glucocorticoid use | Osteoporosis | DEXA regardless of age/sex |

## Quick Reference: When to Screen What

### By Decade of Life

#### Age 18-29
- BP annually
- Depression annually
- Anxiety annually (through 64)
- Chlamydia/Gonorrhea annually (females <= 24, sexually active)
- Pap smear at 21, then every 3 years (females with cervix)
- HIV at least once
- Hepatitis B/C one-time
- HPV vaccine (if not completed)
- Tdap (if not received, then Td q10y)
- Annual flu vaccine

#### Age 30-39
- Everything above
- Pap + HPV cotesting every 5 years (age 30+, females)
- Diabetes screening if overweight (age 35+)
- Hepatitis B vaccine (if not immunized)

#### Age 40-49
- Everything above
- Mammography every 2 years (females, starting at 40)
- Lipid panel every 5 years
- CVD risk assessment (statin consideration)
- Colorectal cancer screening starting at 45

#### Age 50-64
- Everything above
- Lung cancer LDCT annually (if 20+ pack-year smoker)
- Shingrix 2-dose series (age 50+)
- Continue colorectal cancer screening
- Continue mammography
- Continue cervical screening through 65

#### Age 65-74
- BP annually
- Depression annually
- Colorectal cancer screening
- Mammography every 2 years (females, through 74)
- Osteoporosis DEXA (females 65+)
- AAA ultrasound one-time (males who ever smoked)
- Pneumococcal PCV20 (one-time)
- Shingrix (if not completed)
- Stop cervical screening (if adequate prior screening)
- Influenza annually (high-dose or adjuvanted preferred 65+)

#### Age 75+
- BP annually
- Depression annually
- Colorectal 76-85 (selective, shared decision)
- Stop mammography at 75 (insufficient evidence beyond)
- Stop lung LDCT at 81
- Continue immunizations
- Osteoporosis (continue if prior osteopenia/osteoporosis)
