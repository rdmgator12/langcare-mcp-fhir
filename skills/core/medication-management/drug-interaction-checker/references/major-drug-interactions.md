# Major Drug-Drug Interaction Reference

Comprehensive list of clinically significant drug-drug interaction pairs organized by category, severity, mechanism, and clinical significance. Use for cross-referencing active medication pairs.

## Severity Classification

| Level | Definition | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Contraindicated | Should never be given together | Do not co-prescribe. Find alternative. |
| Major | May be life-threatening or cause permanent damage | Avoid combination or implement strict monitoring. |
| Moderate | May cause deterioration in clinical status | Modify therapy or increase monitoring. |
| Minor | Limited clinical effects | Be aware. Usually no change needed. |

## Anticoagulant Interactions

### Warfarin (RxNorm: 11289)

| Interacting Drug | Severity | Mechanism | Clinical Effect | Management |
|-----------------|----------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) | Major | Additive antiplatelet + GI erosion | Increased bleeding risk, GI hemorrhage | Avoid. Use acetaminophen. If unavoidable, add PPI. |
| Aspirin (high dose) | Major | Additive antiplatelet | Increased bleeding risk | Use only if cardiology-indicated. Add PPI. |
| Amiodarone | Major | CYP2C9 inhibition | INR increase 50-70% | Reduce warfarin 30-50%. Monitor INR weekly x4. |
| Fluconazole | Major | CYP2C9 inhibition | INR increase, bleeding | Reduce warfarin dose. Monitor INR at days 3, 7. |
| Metronidazole | Major | CYP2C9 inhibition | INR increase | Monitor INR. Short courses (7-10 days) may need warfarin reduction. |
| Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole | Major | CYP2C9 inhibition + protein displacement | INR increase, bleeding | Reduce warfarin 25-50%. Monitor INR at day 3-5. |
| Rifampin | Major | CYP2C9/3A4 induction | INR decrease, clot risk | Increase warfarin dose 2-3x. Monitor INR closely. Alternative: use rifabutin. |
| Phenytoin | Major | Complex (initial increase then decrease) | Unpredictable INR changes | Avoid. Use alternative anticoagulant or antiepileptic. |
| Cranberry juice (large quantities) | Moderate | CYP2C9 inhibition (uncertain) | INR increase | Limit intake. Monitor INR. |
| Vitamin K (high intake) | Moderate | Pharmacodynamic antagonism | Decreased anticoagulation | Maintain consistent vitamin K intake. |

### DOACs (Apixaban, Rivaroxaban, Dabigatran, Edoxaban)

| Interacting Drug | Severity | Mechanism | Clinical Effect | Management |
|-----------------|----------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors + P-gp inhibitors (ketoconazole, itraconazole, ritonavir) | Contraindicated (with dabigatran) / Major | Reduced metabolism and efflux | Increased DOAC levels, bleeding | Contraindicated with dabigatran. Reduce apixaban/rivaroxaban dose or avoid. |
| Strong CYP3A4 inducers (rifampin, carbamazepine, phenytoin) | Major | Increased metabolism | Reduced DOAC levels, clot risk | Avoid combination with all DOACs. |
| NSAIDs | Major | Additive bleeding risk | Increased bleeding | Avoid. Use acetaminophen. |
| Antiplatelet agents (clopidogrel, prasugrel) | Major | Additive bleeding risk | Increased bleeding | Use only if cardiology-indicated (e.g., recent stent). Shortest duration possible. |
| Dronedarone | Major | P-gp inhibition | Increased dabigatran levels | Contraindicated with dabigatran. Caution with others. |

## Cardiovascular Interactions

### ACE Inhibitors / ARBs

| Interacting Drug | Severity | Mechanism | Clinical Effect | Management |
|-----------------|----------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| Potassium supplements | Major | Additive hyperkalemia | Hyperkalemia (arrhythmia risk) | Monitor K+ closely. Avoid routine K+ supplementation. |
| Spironolactone/Eplerenone | Major | Additive hyperkalemia | Hyperkalemia | Monitor K+ within 1 week, then monthly. Avoid if K+ >5.0. |
| Trimethoprim | Moderate | Additive hyperkalemia | Hyperkalemia | Monitor K+ during concurrent use. |
| NSAIDs | Moderate | Reduced renal prostaglandins | Decreased antihypertensive effect + acute kidney injury | Avoid chronic NSAID use. Monitor creatinine. |
| Aliskiren | Contraindicated (in diabetes/CKD) | Dual RAAS blockade | Hyperkalemia, renal failure, hypotension | Contraindicated in diabetes or eGFR <60. |
| Lithium | Moderate | Reduced renal lithium clearance | Lithium toxicity | Monitor lithium levels. May need dose reduction. |

### Digoxin (RxNorm: 3407)

| Interacting Drug | Severity | Mechanism | Clinical Effect | Management |
|-----------------|----------|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| Amiodarone | Major | P-gp inhibition + renal clearance reduction | Digoxin toxicity (nausea, arrhythmia) | Reduce digoxin dose by 50%. Monitor levels. |
| Verapamil | Major | P-gp inhibition | Increased digoxin levels + additive AV block | Reduce digoxin 25-50%. Monitor levels and HR. |
| Quinidine | Major | P-gp inhibition | Digoxin levels double | Reduce digoxin by 50%. Monitor levels. |
| Dronedarone | Major | P-gp inhibition | Increased digoxin levels | Reduce digoxin dose. Monitor levels. |
| Diuretics (K+-wasting) | Moderate | Hypokalemia increases digoxin sensitivity | Digoxin toxicity at therapeutic levels | Monitor K+. Supplement potassium. |
| Clarithromycin/Erythromycin | Major | P-gp inhibition + gut bacteria alteration | Increased digoxin levels | Monitor digoxin levels. Consider azithromycin alternative. |

### QT-Prolonging Drug Combinations

| Drug A | Drug B | Severity | Management |
|--------|--------|----------|------------|
| Amiodarone | Fluoroquinolones (levofloxacin, moxifloxacin) | Contraindicated | Use alternative antibiotic (azithromycin is lower risk). |
| Sotalol | Fluoroquinolones | Contraindicated | Avoid combination. |
| Amiodarone | Ondansetron | Major | Use alternative antiemetic (granisetron has less QT effect). |
| Haloperidol | Methadone | Major | Monitor QTc. Limit doses. |
| Citalopram/Escitalopram | Erythromycin | Major | Use alternative SSRI (sertraline) or antibiotic (azithromycin). |
| Any 2+ QT-prolonging drugs | (additive) | Major | Obtain baseline ECG. Monitor QTc. Correct electrolytes. |

## CNS / Psychotropic Interactions

### Serotonin Syndrome Risk Combinations

| Drug A | Drug B | Severity | Mechanism |
|--------|--------|----------|-----------|
| MAOIs (phenelzine, tranylcypromine, selegiline) | SSRIs or SNRIs | Contraindicated | Excessive serotonin accumulation. 14-day washout required. |
| MAOIs | Meperidine | Contraindicated | Serotonin syndrome, hypertensive crisis |
| MAOIs | Tramadol | Contraindicated | Serotonin syndrome |
| SSRIs | Tramadol | Major | Serotonin syndrome risk + seizure threshold lowering |
| SSRIs | Triptans (sumatriptan) | Moderate | Serotonin syndrome (risk generally low but FDA warning exists) |
| SSRIs/SNRIs | Linezolid | Contraindicated | Linezolid is a weak MAOI. Serotonin syndrome. |
| SSRIs/SNRIs | Methylene blue IV | Contraindicated | MAOI activity of methylene blue. |
| Lithium | SSRIs | Moderate | Increased serotonin effect | Monitor for tremor, hyperreflexia, diarrhea. |

**Serotonin Syndrome Signs**: Agitation, tremor, hyperreflexia, clonus, hyperthermia, diaphoresis, diarrhea, altered mental status.

## Antibiotic Interactions

| Drug A | Drug B | Severity | Mechanism | Effect |
|--------|--------|----------|-----------|--------|
| Fluoroquinolones | Divalent/trivalent cations (antacids, calcium, iron, sucralfate) | Major | Chelation, reduced absorption | Antibiotic failure | Separate by 2 hours before or 6 hours after. |
| Tetracyclines | Divalent cations (same as above) | Major | Chelation | Antibiotic failure | Separate by 2-3 hours. |
| Methotrexate | Trimethoprim | Major | Additive folate antagonism | Pancytopenia | Avoid. If necessary, supplement leucovorin. |
| Methotrexate | NSAIDs | Major | Reduced renal MTX clearance | Methotrexate toxicity | Avoid NSAIDs or hold around MTX dosing days. |
| Clarithromycin | Statins (simvastatin, lovastatin) | Contraindicated | CYP3A4 inhibition | Rhabdomyolysis | Use azithromycin instead. Or hold statin during course. |
| Clarithromycin | Colchicine (with renal impairment) | Contraindicated | CYP3A4 + P-gp inhibition | Fatal colchicine toxicity | Use azithromycin alternative. |
| Rifampin | Oral contraceptives | Major | CYP3A4 induction | Contraceptive failure | Use alternative contraception during and 28 days after. |
| Rifampin | HIV protease inhibitors | Contraindicated | Massive CYP3A4 induction | Subtherapeutic PI levels | Use rifabutin instead. |

## Endocrine Interactions

### Insulin / Sulfonylureas

| Interacting Drug | Severity | Mechanism | Effect | Management |
|-----------------|----------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Beta-blockers (non-selective) | Moderate | Mask hypoglycemia symptoms + impair gluconeogenesis | Prolonged unrecognized hypoglycemia | Use cardioselective beta-blocker (metoprolol, bisoprolol). |
| Fluoroquinolones | Moderate | Dysglycemia (both hypo- and hyperglycemia) | Unpredictable glucose changes | Monitor glucose closely during antibiotic course. |
| Corticosteroids | Moderate | Insulin resistance | Hyperglycemia | Increase insulin/sulfonylurea dose. Monitor glucose. |

### Thyroid Medications (Levothyroxine)

| Interacting Drug | Severity | Mechanism | Management |
|-----------------|----------|-----------|------------|
| Calcium supplements | Moderate | Chelation, reduced absorption | Separate by 4 hours. |
| Iron supplements | Moderate | Chelation, reduced absorption | Separate by 4 hours. |
| PPIs/H2-blockers | Moderate | Reduced gastric acid, impaired absorption | Monitor TSH. May need dose increase. |
| Cholestyramine | Major | Bile acid binding, reduced absorption | Separate by 4-6 hours. |
| Warfarin | Moderate | Levothyroxine increases warfarin sensitivity | Monitor INR when thyroid dose changes. |

## Immunosuppressant Interactions

| Drug A | Drug B | Severity | Mechanism | Effect |
|--------|--------|----------|-----------|--------|
| Cyclosporine/Tacrolimus | Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (azole antifungals, macrolides) | Major | CYP3A4 inhibition | Increased immunosuppressant levels, nephrotoxicity | Monitor drug levels. Use fluconazole cautiously (dose-dependent). |
| Cyclosporine/Tacrolimus | Strong CYP3A4 inducers (rifampin, phenytoin, carbamazepine) | Major | CYP3A4 induction | Subtherapeutic levels, rejection | Avoid. Monitor levels if unavoidable. |
| Cyclosporine | Statins (atorvastatin, simvastatin) | Major | CYP3A4 inhibition by cyclosporine | Rhabdomyolysis | Use pravastatin, fluvastatin, or rosuvastatin (max 5mg). |
| Methotrexate | Penicillins | Moderate | Reduced renal MTX clearance | Methotrexate toxicity | Monitor MTX levels and CBC. |
| Mycophenolate | Antacids (Mg/Al) | Moderate | Reduced absorption | Decreased mycophenolate levels | Separate by 2 hours. |

## Interaction Evidence Levels

| Level | Definition | Frequency |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Established | Proven in controlled studies | Occurs in most patients |
| Probable | Very likely based on pharmacology + case reports | Occurs frequently |
| Suspected | Limited data but pharmacologically plausible | May occur in susceptible patients |
| Possible | Theoretical or isolated case reports | Uncommon |
