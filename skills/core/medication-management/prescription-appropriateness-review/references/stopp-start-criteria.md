# STOPP/START Criteria Reference

Source: STOPP/START Criteria Version 3 (Screening Tool of Older Persons' Prescriptions / Screening Tool to Alert to Right Treatment). Applicable to patients aged 65 years and older.

---

## STOPP Criteria: Potentially Inappropriate Prescriptions

### Section A: Indication of Drug

| Code | Criterion |
|------|----------|
| A1 | Any drug prescribed without an evidence-based clinical indication |
| A2 | Any drug prescribed beyond the recommended duration where treatment duration is defined |
| A3 | Any duplicate drug class prescription (e.g., two NSAIDs, two SSRIs, two loop diuretics, two ACE inhibitors) without clinical rationale. Excludes duplicate drugs that may be needed for PRN use (e.g., 2 inhaled beta-2 agonists for asthma/COPD). |

### Section B: Cardiovascular System

| Code | Criterion |
|------|----------|
| B1 | Digoxin for heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (no proven benefit) |
| B2 | Verapamil or diltiazem with NYHA class III-IV heart failure (may worsen HF) |
| B3 | Beta-blocker with bradycardia (<50 bpm), type II second-degree or third-degree heart block |
| B4 | Amiodarone as first-line therapy for supraventricular tachyarrhythmias (higher risk of side effects than beta-blockers, digoxin, verapamil, or diltiazem) |
| B5 | Loop diuretic as first-line treatment for hypertension (safer, more effective alternatives exist) |
| B6 | Loop diuretic for dependent ankle edema without clinical, biochemical, or radiological evidence of heart failure, liver failure, nephrotic syndrome, or renal failure |
| B7 | Centrally-acting antihypertensives (methyldopa, clonidine, moxonidine, rilmenidine, guanfacine) unless clear intolerance or lack of efficacy with other classes |
| B8 | ACE inhibitor or ARB in patients with hyperkalemia (K+ >5.5 mEq/L) |
| B9 | Aldosterone antagonists (spironolactone, eplerenone) with concurrent potassium-sparing drugs without monitoring of serum potassium |
| B10 | Phosphodiesterase type-5 inhibitors in severe heart failure with hypotension (SBP <90 mmHg) |
| B11 | Antiplatelet agents with concurrent significant bleeding risk (e.g., severe thrombocytopenia, recent GI bleeding, concurrent anticoagulant) |

### Section C: Antiplatelet / Anticoagulant Drugs

| Code | Criterion |
|------|----------|
| C1 | Long-term aspirin at doses >160mg/day (increased bleeding risk, no evidence of increased efficacy above 75-160mg) |
| C2 | Aspirin with history of peptic ulcer disease without PPI co-therapy |
| C3 | Aspirin or clopidogrel with no history of cardiovascular, cerebrovascular, or peripheral vascular disease (not indicated for primary prevention routinely) |
| C4 | Aspirin to treat dizziness not clearly attributable to cerebrovascular disease |
| C5 | Anticoagulant plus antiplatelet(s) for stroke prevention without concurrent acute coronary syndrome or recent (<12 months) coronary stent |
| C6 | Ticlopidine (clopidogrel is equally effective with fewer side effects) |
| C7 | Anticoagulant at higher than recommended therapeutic dose (e.g., warfarin without INR monitoring) |

### Section D: Central Nervous System and Psychotropic Drugs

| Code | Criterion |
|------|----------|
| D1 | Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs) in patients with dementia, narrow-angle glaucoma, cardiac conduction abnormalities, prostatism, or prior history of urinary retention |
| D2 | Initiation of TCAs as first-line antidepressant (higher risk of adverse effects than SSRIs/SNRIs) |
| D3 | Neuroleptics with anticholinergic properties in patients with prostatism or history of urinary retention |
| D4 | SSRIs with current or recent significant hyponatremia (Na+ <130 mEq/L) |
| D5 | Benzodiazepines for >=4 weeks (no indication for longer treatment; risk of sedation, confusion, falls, road traffic accidents; all benzodiazepines should be withdrawn gradually if taken >4 weeks due to risk of withdrawal syndrome) |
| D6 | Antipsychotics (other than quetiapine or clozapine) in those with parkinsonism or Lewy body dementia (risk of severe extrapyramidal symptoms) |
| D7 | Anticholinergics/antimuscarinics to treat extrapyramidal side effects of antipsychotics (risk of anticholinergic toxicity) |
| D8 | Anticholinergics in patients with dementia or chronic cognitive impairment (risk of worsening cognition) |
| D9 | Neuroleptic/antipsychotic as hypnotic unless sleep disturbance is due to psychosis or dementia |
| D10 | Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors with concurrent drugs that reduce heart rate (beta-blockers, digoxin, diltiazem, verapamil) - risk of cardiac conduction failure, syncope, injury |
| D11 | Phenothiazines as first-line treatment (sedation, movement disorder risk) |
| D12 | Levodopa or dopamine agonists for benign essential tremor (no evidence of benefit) |
| D13 | First-generation antihistamines (sedation, anticholinergic effects, safer alternatives exist) |

### Section E: Renal System

| Code | Criterion |
|------|----------|
| E1 | Digoxin at long-term dose >125mcg/day with eGFR <30 mL/min (risk of toxicity) |
| E2 | Direct thrombin inhibitors (dabigatran) with eGFR <30 mL/min (risk of bleeding) |
| E3 | Factor Xa inhibitors (rivaroxaban, apixaban) with eGFR <15 mL/min (risk of bleeding) |
| E4 | NSAIDs with eGFR <50 mL/min (risk of further renal impairment) |
| E5 | Colchicine with eGFR <10 mL/min (risk of colchicine toxicity) |
| E6 | Metformin with eGFR <30 mL/min (risk of lactic acidosis) |

### Section F: Gastrointestinal System

| Code | Criterion |
|------|----------|
| F1 | PPI for uncomplicated peptic ulcer disease or erosive peptic esophagitis at full therapeutic dose for >8 weeks |
| F2 | Prochlorperazine or metoclopramide with parkinsonism (risk of exacerbating parkinsonism) |
| F3 | PPI with C. difficile infection (increased risk of recurrence) |

### Section G: Respiratory System

| Code | Criterion |
|------|----------|
| G1 | Theophylline as monotherapy for COPD (safer, more effective alternatives) |
| G2 | Systemic corticosteroids instead of inhaled for COPD maintenance (unnecessary systemic side effect exposure) |
| G3 | Non-selective beta-blockers in COPD or asthma (risk of bronchospasm) |
| G4 | Benzodiazepines with acute or chronic respiratory failure (risk of respiratory depression) |

### Section H: Musculoskeletal System

| Code | Criterion |
|------|----------|
| H1 | Long-term NSAID (>3 months) for symptom relief of osteoarthritis where paracetamol has not been tried |
| H2 | Long-term corticosteroids (>3 months) as monotherapy for rheumatoid arthritis |
| H3 | Long-term NSAID or colchicine (>3 months) for chronic gout where no contraindication to a xanthine-oxidase inhibitor (allopurinol, febuxostat) |
| H4 | COX-2 selective NSAID with concurrent cardiovascular disease |
| H5 | NSAID with concurrent corticosteroid without PPI (high GI bleeding risk) |
| H6 | Oral bisphosphonate in patients unable to sit/stand upright for 30 minutes (esophageal erosion risk) |

### Section K: Drugs that Increase Falls Risk

| Code | Criterion |
|------|----------|
| K1 | Benzodiazepines (sedation, impaired balance, slow reaction time) |
| K2 | Neuroleptics/antipsychotics (sedation, gait dyspraxia, extrapyramidal symptoms) |
| K3 | Vasodilator drugs known to cause persistent postural hypotension (documented recurrent >20mmHg drop in SBP) |
| K4 | Z-drugs (zolpidem, zopiclone, zaleplon) - similar falls risk as benzodiazepines |

## START Criteria: Potential Prescribing Omissions

### Section A: Cardiovascular System

| Code | Criterion | Condition Present | Medication to Consider |
|------|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| A1 | Vitamin K antagonist or DOAC in chronic atrial fibrillation | Atrial fibrillation | Warfarin (INR 2-3) or DOAC |
| A2 | Aspirin (75-160mg) in chronic atrial fibrillation where VKA/DOAC is contraindicated | AFib + anticoagulant contraindicated | Aspirin |
| A3 | Antiplatelet therapy in documented coronary, cerebral, or peripheral vascular disease | Documented atherosclerotic disease | Aspirin 75-160mg or clopidogrel |
| A4 | Antihypertensive therapy where SBP consistently >160mmHg and/or DBP consistently >90mmHg | Hypertension (uncontrolled) | ACE/ARB, CCB, or thiazide |
| A5 | Statin therapy in documented coronary, cerebral, or peripheral vascular disease | Atherosclerotic CVD | Statin (atorvastatin or rosuvastatin) |
| A6 | ACE inhibitor in systolic heart failure and/or documented coronary artery disease | HFrEF or CAD | ACE inhibitor (or ARB if intolerant) |
| A7 | Beta-blocker in ischemic heart disease | Stable CAD, post-MI | Beta-blocker |
| A8 | Beta-blocker in stable systolic heart failure | HFrEF | Carvedilol, bisoprolol, or metoprolol succinate |

### Section B: Respiratory System

| Code | Criterion | Condition Present | Medication to Consider |
|------|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| B1 | Regular inhaled beta-2 agonist or anticholinergic for mild-moderate asthma or COPD | Asthma/COPD | Inhaled bronchodilator |
| B2 | Regular inhaled corticosteroid for moderate-severe asthma or COPD with FEV1 <50% | Asthma/COPD (moderate-severe) | Inhaled corticosteroid |
| B3 | Continuous home oxygen with documented chronic hypoxemia (pO2 <55mmHg or SaO2 <88%) | Chronic respiratory failure | Home O2 |

### Section C: Central Nervous System

| Code | Criterion | Condition Present | Medication to Consider |
|------|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| C1 | Levodopa or a dopamine agonist in idiopathic Parkinson disease with functional impairment | Parkinson disease | Levodopa or dopamine agonist |
| C2 | Non-TCA antidepressant in presence of persistent major depressive symptoms | Major depression | SSRI, SNRI, or other non-TCA |

### Section D: Gastrointestinal System

| Code | Criterion | Condition Present | Medication to Consider |
|------|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| D1 | PPI with severe GERD or peptic stricture requiring dilation | Severe GERD, peptic stricture | PPI |
| D2 | Fiber supplement for chronic symptomatic diverticular disease with constipation | Diverticular disease + constipation | Fiber supplement |

### Section E: Musculoskeletal System

| Code | Criterion | Condition Present | Medication to Consider |
|------|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| E1 | DMARD in active moderate-severe RA | Rheumatoid arthritis | Methotrexate, hydroxychloroquine, etc. |
| E2 | Bisphosphonate and vitamin D/calcium in patients on maintenance corticosteroid therapy | Chronic corticosteroid use | Bisphosphonate + vitamin D + calcium |
| E3 | Vitamin D and calcium supplement in osteoporosis | Osteoporosis (documented or high risk) | Vitamin D + calcium |
| E4 | Bone anti-resorptive or anabolic therapy in osteoporosis where no contraindication | Osteoporosis with fracture risk | Bisphosphonate or denosumab |
| E5 | Xanthine-oxidase inhibitor (allopurinol, febuxostat) in recurrent gout | Recurrent gout episodes | Allopurinol or febuxostat |

### Section F: Endocrine System

| Code | Criterion | Condition Present | Medication to Consider |
|------|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| F1 | ACE inhibitor or ARB in diabetes with proteinuria or microalbuminuria | Diabetes + albuminuria | ACE inhibitor or ARB |
| F2 | Statin in diabetes mellitus if coexisting major cardiovascular risk factors | Diabetes + CV risk | Statin |
| F3 | Antiplatelet therapy in diabetes mellitus with coexisting major cardiovascular risk factors | Diabetes + CV risk | Aspirin 75-160mg |

### Section G: Urogenital System

| Code | Criterion | Condition Present | Medication to Consider |
|------|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| G1 | Alpha-1 blocker for symptomatic prostatism where prostatectomy not indicated | BPH with LUTS | Tamsulosin, alfuzosin |
| G2 | 5-alpha reductase inhibitor for symptomatic prostatism where prostatectomy not indicated | BPH (large prostate) | Finasteride, dutasteride |

### Section H: Analgesics

| Code | Criterion | Condition Present | Medication to Consider |
|------|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| H1 | High-potency opioids in moderate-severe pain where paracetamol, NSAIDs, or low-potency opioids not appropriate or not tolerated | Moderate-severe pain | Appropriate opioid (lowest effective dose) |
| H2 | Laxatives in patients on opioids | Opioid prescription | Docusate, senna, PEG |

### Section I: Vaccines

| Code | Criterion | Condition Present | Medication to Consider |
|------|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| I1 | Annual influenza vaccine | All patients >=65 | Influenza vaccine |
| I2 | Pneumococcal vaccine per national guidelines | All patients >=65 | Pneumococcal vaccine |
