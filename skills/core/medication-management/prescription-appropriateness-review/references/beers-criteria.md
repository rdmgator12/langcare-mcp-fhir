# AGS 2023 Beers Criteria Reference

Source: American Geriatrics Society 2023 Updated AGS Beers Criteria for Potentially Inappropriate Medication Use in Older Adults.

Applies to patients aged 65 years and older. These are recommendations, not absolute prohibitions. Clinical judgment should consider the individual patient's condition, preferences, and available alternatives.

## Table 1: Medications Potentially Inappropriate in Older Adults

### Anticholinergics

| Medication | Rationale | Recommendation | Evidence | Strength |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| First-generation antihistamines (chlorpheniramine, cyproheptadine, dimenhydrinate, diphenhydramine, doxylamine, hydroxyzine, meclizine, promethazine) | Highly anticholinergic. Clearance reduced with age. Sedation, confusion, falls, urinary retention, constipation. Tolerance to sleep effects develops within 3 days. | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |
| Antispasmodics (belladonna, clidinium-chlordiazepoxide, dicyclomine, hyoscyamine, propantheline, scopolamine) | Highly anticholinergic, uncertain effectiveness at doses tolerated by older adults | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |

### Antithrombotics

| Medication | Rationale | Recommendation | Evidence | Strength |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| Dipyridamole, oral short-acting | Orthostatic hypotension. More effective alternatives available. | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |
| Ticlopidine | More effective and safer alternatives (clopidogrel) available | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |

### Anti-infectives

| Medication | Rationale | Recommendation | Evidence | Strength |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| Nitrofurantoin | Pulmonary toxicity, hepatotoxicity, peripheral neuropathy, especially with prolonged use. Avoid if CrCl <30 mL/min due to lack of efficacy. | Avoid long-term suppression. Avoid if CrCl <30. | Low | Strong |

### Cardiovascular

| Medication | Rationale | Recommendation | Evidence | Strength |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| Peripheral alpha-1 blockers for hypertension (doxazosin, prazosin, terazosin) | High risk of orthostatic hypotension. Not recommended as routine treatment for hypertension. | Avoid as antihypertensive | Moderate | Strong |
| Central alpha-agonists (clonidine, guanabenz, guanfacine, methyldopa) | High risk of CNS adverse effects. May cause bradycardia and orthostatic hypotension. | Avoid as first-line antihypertensive | Low | Strong |
| Nifedipine, immediate-release | Hypotension, risk of precipitating myocardial ischemia | Avoid | High | Strong |
| Amiodarone | First-line use for atrial fibrillation should be avoided unless heart failure or substantial LVH present. Thyroid, pulmonary, QT, and other toxicities. | Avoid as first-line for AFib unless HF present | High | Strong |
| Dronedarone | Worse outcomes in permanent AFib and severe HF (NYHA IV or recent decompensation). | Avoid in permanent AFib or severe HF | High | Strong |
| Digoxin (initiation dose >0.125mg/day) | Increased risk of toxicity in older adults with decreased renal clearance. | Avoid initiating >0.125mg/day | Moderate | Strong |

### Central Nervous System

| Medication | Rationale | Recommendation | Evidence | Strength |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| Antidepressants (amitriptyline, clomipramine, desipramine, doxepin >6mg, imipramine, nortriptyline, paroxetine, protriptyline, trimipramine) | Highly anticholinergic, sedating, orthostatic hypotension. Paroxetine is most anticholinergic SSRI. | Avoid | High | Strong |
| Antipsychotics (first and second generation) | Increased risk of stroke and greater rate of cognitive decline and mortality in persons with dementia. Avoid for behavioral problems of dementia unless non-pharmacologic options failed and patient threatens self or others. | Avoid, especially in dementia | Moderate | Strong |
| Benzodiazepines (short, intermediate, and long-acting) | Cognitive impairment, delirium, falls, fractures, motor vehicle crashes. Older adults have increased sensitivity. | Avoid | High | Strong |
| Nonbenzodiazepine hypnotics (eszopiclone, zaleplon, zolpidem) | Similar adverse effects as benzodiazepines (falls, delirium, fractures). Minimal improvement in sleep latency and duration. ER/MR visits, hospitalizations. | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |
| Meprobamate | High rate of physical dependence, sedation | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |
| Ergot mesylates, isoxsuprine | Lack of efficacy | Avoid | High | Strong |

### Endocrine

| Medication | Rationale | Recommendation | Evidence | Strength |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| Sulfonylureas, long-acting (glyburide/glibenclamide, chlorpropamide) | Higher risk of severe prolonged hypoglycemia in older adults | Avoid | High | Strong |
| Megestrol | Minimal effect on weight, increases thrombotic events, possibly death | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |
| Testosterone (without confirmed hypogonadism) | Potential cardiac risks, prescribe only if clinical symptoms + confirmed low levels | Avoid unless indicated | Moderate | Weak |
| Desiccated thyroid | Cardiac concerns, safer alternatives available | Avoid | Low | Strong |
| Growth hormone | Minimal effect on body composition, edema, arthralgia, carpal tunnel, gynecomastia, glucose intolerance | Avoid except for confirmed GH deficiency | High | Strong |

### Gastrointestinal

| Medication | Rationale | Recommendation | Evidence | Strength |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| Metoclopramide | Extrapyramidal effects, tardive dyskinesia. Risk higher in frail older adults. | Avoid unless gastroparesis | Moderate | Strong |
| Mineral oil (oral) | Aspiration risk, adverse GI effects | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |

### Pain

| Medication | Rationale | Recommendation | Evidence | Strength |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| Meperidine | Neurotoxic metabolite (normeperidine) accumulates, seizure risk. Not effective orally at commonly used doses. | Avoid | High | Strong |
| Non-COX-selective NSAIDs (chronic use) | Increased risk of GI bleeding/peptic ulcer, AKI, fluid retention, hypertension exacerbation. Risk increases further with concurrent anticoagulants, antiplatelet agents, corticosteroids. | Avoid chronic use. Use topical alternatives when possible. | Moderate | Strong |
| Indomethacin | Most CNS adverse effects of all NSAIDs | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |
| Ketorolac (oral, parenteral) | High GI bleeding risk | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |
| Skeletal muscle relaxants (carisoprodol, chlorzoxazone, cyclobenzaprine, metaxalone, methocarbamol, orphenadrine) | Anticholinergic effects, sedation, falls risk. Poorly tolerated by older adults. Effectiveness at doses tolerated by older adults questionable. | Avoid | Moderate | Strong |

### Genitourinary

| Medication | Rationale | Recommendation | Evidence | Strength |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------|----------|
| Desmopressin for nocturia | High risk of hyponatremia. Na+ must be monitored. | Avoid for nocturia | Moderate | Strong |

## Table 2: Drug-Disease/Drug-Syndrome Interactions to Avoid

### Heart Failure

| Avoid | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| NSAIDs, COX-2 inhibitors | Fluid retention, exacerbate HF |
| Nondihydropyridine CCBs (diltiazem, verapamil) for HFrEF | Negative inotropic effects, worsen HF |
| Thiazolidinediones (pioglitazone, rosiglitazone) | Fluid retention |
| Cilostazol | Contraindicated in HF (any severity) |
| Dronedarone (severe HF) | Increased mortality |

### Syncope

| Avoid | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine) | Bradycardia, syncope risk |
| Peripheral alpha-blockers (doxazosin, prazosin, terazosin) | Orthostatic hypotension |
| TCAs, chlorpromazine, thioridazine, olanzapine | Orthostatic hypotension |

### Dementia / Cognitive Impairment

| Avoid | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| Anticholinergics (see Table 1 anticholinergic list) | Worsen cognitive function |
| Benzodiazepines | Worsen cognition, increase delirium risk |
| Z-drugs (zolpidem, eszopiclone, zaleplon) | Delirium risk |
| Antipsychotics (chronic use) | Increased mortality, cerebrovascular events |
| H2-receptor antagonists (famotidine, ranitidine) | May worsen cognition in patients with dementia |

### Falls / Fracture History

| Avoid unless alternatives unavailable | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| Anticonvulsants (except for seizure disorder) | Ataxia, impaired psychomotor function |
| Antipsychotics | Sedation, gait disturbance |
| Benzodiazepines | Sedation, impaired balance |
| Nonbenzodiazepine hypnotics | Same as benzodiazepines |
| Opioids | Sedation, impaired balance |
| SSRIs/SNRIs | Hyponatremia, falls (especially initial weeks) |
| TCAs | Orthostatic hypotension, sedation |

### Parkinson Disease

| Avoid | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| All antipsychotics (except quetiapine, clozapine, pimavanserin) | Worsen parkinsonian symptoms |
| Metoclopramide, prochlorperazine, promethazine | Dopamine-blocking, worsen parkinsonism |

### Chronic Kidney Disease (eGFR <30)

| Avoid | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| NSAIDs | AKI exacerbation, further renal damage |
| Nitrofurantoin | Ineffective, peripheral neuropathy risk |

### Urinary Incontinence (all types) in Women

| Avoid | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| Estrogen (oral and transdermal, NOT vaginal) | Worsens incontinence |
| Alpha-blockers (prazosin, terazosin, doxazosin) | Urethral relaxation, stress incontinence |

### Lower Urinary Tract Symptoms / BPH

| Avoid | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| Strongly anticholinergic drugs | Urinary retention |
| Inhaled anticholinergics | May exacerbate |

### Chronic Constipation

| Avoid | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| Oral antimuscarinics for urinary incontinence (oxybutynin, tolterodine, fesoterodine, solifenacin) | Worsen constipation |
| Nondihydropyridine CCBs (diltiazem, verapamil) | Constipating |
| First-generation antihistamines (diphenhydramine, chlorpheniramine) | Anticholinergic constipation |
| Clonidine | Constipation |

### Delirium

| Avoid | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|
| Anticholinergics | Precipitate or worsen delirium |
| Benzodiazepines | Precipitate or worsen delirium (except for alcohol/BZD withdrawal) |
| Corticosteroids | Can precipitate delirium |
| H2-receptor antagonists | May contribute to delirium |
| Meperidine | Neurotoxic, delirium risk |
| Nonbenzodiazepine hypnotics | Delirium risk |

## Table 3: Medications to Use with Caution

| Medication | Rationale | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Aspirin for primary prevention (age >=70) | Increased bleeding risk without clear CVD benefit in oldest adults | Consider risk-benefit |
| Dabigatran | Higher risk of GI bleeding vs warfarin in adults >=75 | Use with caution, especially if eGFR 15-30 |
| Prasugrel | Increased bleeding risk in age >=75 | Generally avoid in >=75 |
| SSRIs | Risk of hyponatremia (SIADH), falls (especially first weeks) | Monitor Na+ within 2 weeks of initiation |
| SNRIs | Similar to SSRIs: hyponatremia, falls | Monitor Na+ |
| Diuretics | Electrolyte disturbance, falls | Monitor electrolytes |
| Tramadol | Hyponatremia (SIADH), seizures, serotonin syndrome with SSRIs | Avoid with SSRIs/SNRIs if possible |
| PPIs (>8 weeks) | C. difficile, fractures, hypomagnesemia, B12 deficiency, CKD | Periodically re-evaluate need |

## Table 4: Drug-Drug Interactions to Avoid in Older Adults

| Drug A | Drug B | Risk | Action |
|--------|--------|------|--------|
| ACE inhibitor or ARB | Potassium-sparing diuretic | Hyperkalemia | Monitor K+ if combination needed |
| Opioid | Benzodiazepine | Respiratory depression | Avoid combination. FDA boxed warning. |
| Opioid | Gabapentinoid | Respiratory depression | Avoid or use lowest effective doses |
| 2+ CNS-active drugs (opioids + BZDs + antipsychotics + antidepressants + antiepileptics) | Falls, fractures, delirium | Minimize number. Review regularly. |
| Theophylline | Cimetidine | Increased theophylline levels | Use alternative H2 blocker or PPI |
| Warfarin | Amiodarone | Increased INR/bleeding | Reduce warfarin 30-50%, monitor INR |
| Warfarin | NSAIDs | Increased bleeding | Avoid NSAIDs. Use acetaminophen. |
| Lithium | ACE inhibitor or loop diuretic | Lithium toxicity | Monitor lithium level, renal function |
