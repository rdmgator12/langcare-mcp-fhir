# ISMP High-Alert Medications Reference

Source: Institute for Safe Medication Practices (ISMP) High-Alert Medications in Acute Care Settings and Community/Ambulatory Settings.

## Definition

High-alert medications are drugs that bear a heightened risk of causing significant patient harm when used in error. Errors with these medications may not be more common, but the consequences are more devastating.

## Safety Strategies for High-Alert Medications

### Independent Double Checks
Required for:
- Insulin administration
- Heparin infusions
- Chemotherapy
- Patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) pumps
- Epidural infusions

### Tall Man Lettering
Applied to look-alike/sound-alike pairs:
- HYDROmorphone vs HYDROcodone
- EPINEPHrine vs ePHEDrine
- DOBUTamine vs DOPamine
- OxyCODONE vs OxyMORphone
- vinCRIStine vs vinBLAStine

### Standard Concentrations
- Heparin drip: 25,000 units/500mL D5W
- Insulin drip: 100 units/100mL NS
- Vasopressor drips: per institutional protocol

### Maximum Dose Limits
- Morphine: 10mg IV single dose (typical adult)
- HYDROmorphone: 2mg IV single dose (typical adult)
- FentaNYL: 100mcg IV single dose (typical adult, non-tolerant)
- Insulin: verify any single dose >10 units for accuracy
- Heparin bolus: verify any dose >10,000 units
- Warfarin: verify any dose >10mg

## Reconciliation Implications

When a high-alert medication appears during reconciliation:
1. Verify indication against active Condition list
2. Confirm dose is within therapeutic range
3. Check for duplicate therapy within same class
4. Verify allergy/interaction clearance
5. Document verification in reconciliation notes
6. Flag for pharmacist review if any uncertainty
