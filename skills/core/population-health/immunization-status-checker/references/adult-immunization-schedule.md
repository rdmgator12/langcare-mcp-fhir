# CDC 2024 Adult Immunization Schedule

Source: Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP), CDC Recommended Adult Immunization Schedule, United States, 2024.

---

## Routine Vaccines by Age Group

### Influenza (Inactivated or Recombinant)

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 158 | Influenza, injectable, quadrivalent (IIV4) | >= 18 years | 1 dose annually (each season) |
| 185 | Influenza, recombinant, quadrivalent (RIV4) | >= 18 years | 1 dose annually |
| 197 | Influenza, high-dose, quadrivalent (HD-IIV4) | >= 65 years | 1 dose annually (preferred for 65+) |
| 149 | Influenza, live, intranasal (LAIV4) | 18-49 years | 1 dose annually |

**Timing:** Administer in September-October. Can give through end of season (March-April).
**Contraindications:** Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis) to a previous dose or vaccine component. LAIV: immunocompromised, pregnancy, close contacts of severely immunocompromised, received influenza antiviral in prior 48 hours.
**Egg allergy guidance:** Patients with egg allergy of any severity can receive any age-appropriate influenza vaccine. No longer need to use egg-free formulations specifically, but cell-based (ccIIV4, CVX 171) and recombinant (RIV4, CVX 185) are egg-free options.

### COVID-19

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 308 | COVID-19 updated (2024-2025) Pfizer | >= 12 years | 1 dose if previously vaccinated |
| 309 | COVID-19 updated (2024-2025) Moderna | >= 18 years | 1 dose if previously vaccinated |
| 310 | COVID-19 updated (2024-2025) Novavax | >= 12 years | 2 doses if unvaccinated |

**Timing:** Annual updated dose recommended. Immunocompromised may receive additional doses >= 2 months after last dose.
**Contraindications:** Severe allergic reaction to a previous dose or PEG/polysorbate.

### Tdap / Td (Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis)

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 115 | Tdap (Adacel, Boostrix) | >= 19 years | 1 dose Tdap if never received; then Td or Tdap every 10 years |
| 138 | Td | >= 19 years | Booster every 10 years after initial Tdap |

**Special populations:**
- Pregnancy: 1 dose Tdap during each pregnancy (preferably 27-36 weeks gestation)
- Wound management: Td or Tdap if >= 5 years since last tetanus-containing vaccine and wound is contaminated

**Contraindications:** Encephalopathy within 7 days of previous pertussis-containing vaccine (use Td instead of Tdap).

### MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella)

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 03 | MMR | >= 19 years | 1 or 2 doses depending on indication |

**Indications:**
- Born 1957 or later without evidence of immunity: 1 dose
- Healthcare workers, international travelers, college students: 2 doses (28 days apart)
- Born before 1957: generally considered immune (except healthcare workers)

**Contraindications:** Pregnancy, severe immunocompromise (CD4 < 200, active chemo, high-dose steroids), severe allergic reaction to previous dose or neomycin.

### Varicella (Chickenpox)

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 21 | Varicella (Varivax) | >= 19 years | 2 doses (4-8 weeks apart) if no evidence of immunity |

**Evidence of immunity:** Documentation of 2 doses, lab evidence of immunity (IgG), US-born before 1980 (except healthcare workers and pregnant women), or history of disease verified by provider.

**Contraindications:** Pregnancy, severe immunocompromise. Live vaccine -- do not give to immunocompromised patients with CD4 < 200.

### Zoster (Shingles)

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 187 | Recombinant zoster (Shingrix, RZV) | >= 50 years | 2 doses (2-6 months apart) |
| 187 | RZV (immunocompromised) | >= 19 years | 2 doses (1-2 months apart) if immunocompromised |

**Note:** Shingrix is a non-live recombinant vaccine. Safe for immunocompromised patients.
**Previous Zostavax (ZVL):** Revaccinate with Shingrix regardless of prior Zostavax.
**Contraindications:** Severe allergic reaction to previous dose or component. NOT contraindicated in pregnancy (but insufficient data, defer if possible).

### Pneumococcal

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 216 | PCV20 (Prevnar 20) | >= 65 years | 1 dose (preferred) |
| 215 | PCV15 (Vaxneuvance) | >= 65 years | 1 dose PCV15 followed by PPSV23 (>= 1 year later) |
| 33 | PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23) | >= 65 years | See notes |

**Risk-based (age 19-64):**
- Chronic heart/lung/liver disease, diabetes, alcoholism, smoking: 1 dose PCV20 (or PCV15 + PPSV23)
- Immunocompromised, CSF leak, cochlear implant, asplenia: 1 dose PCV20 (or PCV15 + PPSV23 >= 8 weeks later)

**Contraindications:** Severe allergic reaction to previous dose or any component (including diphtheria toxoid for PCV).

### HPV (Human Papillomavirus)

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 165 | HPV9 (Gardasil 9) | 19-26 years | 3 doses (0, 1-2, 6 months) if not previously vaccinated |
| 165 | HPV9 (shared decision) | 27-45 years | 3 doses based on shared clinical decision |

**Note:** If series started before age 15, only 2 doses needed (0, 6-12 months).
**Contraindications:** Severe allergic reaction to previous dose or yeast. Pregnancy (defer, no need to delay if inadvertent).

### Hepatitis A

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 83 | Hepatitis A (Havrix, Vaqta) | >= 19 years | 2 doses (0, 6-12 months) or 3 doses if Twinrix |
| 104 | Hep A + Hep B (Twinrix) | >= 18 years | 3 doses (0, 1, 6 months) |

**Risk-based indications:** Travel to endemic areas, chronic liver disease, clotting factor disorders, homelessness, HIV, MSM, injection drug use, occupational risk.

**Contraindications:** Severe allergic reaction to previous dose or component (neomycin for Havrix).

### Hepatitis B

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 08 | Hepatitis B (Engerix-B, Recombivax HB) | 19-59 years | 3 doses (0, 1, 6 months) -- universal recommendation |
| 189 | Hep B (Heplisav-B) | >= 18 years | 2 doses (0, 1 month) |
| 08 | Hepatitis B (risk-based) | >= 60 years | 2-3 doses if risk factor present |

**Risk factors (age >= 60):** Diabetes, CKD/dialysis, HIV, chronic liver disease, hepatitis C, sexual exposure risk, injection drug use, incarcerated, occupational exposure, household contact of HBsAg+ person.

**Contraindications:** Severe allergic reaction to previous dose or yeast (except Heplisav-B which is yeast-free).

### Meningococcal

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age Group | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----------|----------|
| 167 | MenACWY (Menactra, Menveo) | >= 19 years | Risk-based: 2 doses (8 weeks apart), boost every 5 years |
| 162 | MenB (Bexsero) | 16-23 years | Shared decision: 2 doses (0, >= 1 month) |
| 163 | MenB (Trumenba) | 16-23 years | Shared decision: 2-3 doses |
| 316 | MenABCWY (Penbraya) | 10-25 years | 2 doses |

**Risk-based indications for MenACWY:** Asplenia, complement deficiency, HIV, travel to endemic areas, microbiologists, college students living in dorms (if not vaccinated at 16+), military recruits.

**Risk-based indications for MenB:** Asplenia, complement deficiency, eculizumab/ravulizumab use, microbiologists, outbreak setting.

## Special Populations

### Pregnancy

| Vaccine | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Tdap | 1 dose per pregnancy (27-36 weeks) |
| Influenza (inactivated) | 1 dose (any trimester) |
| COVID-19 | Recommended |
| RSV (maternal, Abrysvo) | 32-36 weeks (Sept-Jan) |
| Hepatitis B | If indicated, safe in pregnancy |
| CONTRAINDICATED (live) | MMR, Varicella, LAIV, Zoster (live) |

### Immunocompromised

| Vaccine | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| All inactivated vaccines | Generally safe; may have reduced immunogenicity |
| Shingrix (RZV) | Recommended >= 19 years (non-live) |
| CONTRAINDICATED (live) | MMR (if CD4 < 200), Varicella (if CD4 < 200), LAIV, Zoster (live - Zostavax) |
| Pneumococcal | PCV20 recommended regardless of age |
| Meningococcal | MenACWY 2-dose series + boosters; MenB if complement deficiency/eculizumab |

### Healthcare Workers

| Vaccine | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Hepatitis B | Required; check anti-HBs titer |
| MMR | 2 doses required regardless of birth year |
| Varicella | 2 doses required if no evidence of immunity |
| Influenza | Annual (institutional requirement) |
| Tdap | 1 dose if not previously received |
| COVID-19 | Recommended (institutional requirement varies) |

## Minimum Intervals Between Doses

| Vaccine | Dose 1 to Dose 2 | Dose 2 to Dose 3 |
|---------|------------------|------------------|
| Hepatitis A | 6 months | -- |
| Hepatitis B (Engerix/Recombivax) | 4 weeks | 8 weeks (and >= 16 weeks from dose 1) |
| Hepatitis B (Heplisav-B) | 4 weeks | -- |
| HPV (3-dose) | 4 weeks | 12 weeks (and >= 5 months from dose 1) |
| MMR | 4 weeks | -- |
| Varicella | 4 weeks | -- |
| Shingrix | 4 weeks (8 weeks preferred) | -- |
| MenACWY | 8 weeks | -- (boost at 5 years if ongoing risk) |
| PCV15 + PPSV23 | >= 8 weeks (immunocompromised) or >= 1 year (immunocompetent) | -- |
