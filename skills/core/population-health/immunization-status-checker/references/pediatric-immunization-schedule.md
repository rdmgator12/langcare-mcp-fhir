# CDC 2024 Pediatric Immunization Schedule

Source: Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP), CDC Recommended Child and Adolescent Immunization Schedule, United States, 2024.

---

## Routine Schedule: Birth Through 18 Years

### Hepatitis B (HepB)

| CVX Code | Dose | Age | Minimum Interval |
|----------|------|-----|-----------------|
| 08 | Dose 1 | Birth (within 24 hours) | -- |
| 08 | Dose 2 | 1 month | 4 weeks from dose 1 |
| 08 | Dose 3 | 6-18 months | 8 weeks from dose 2 AND >= 16 weeks from dose 1 |

**Brand names:** Engerix-B, Recombivax HB
**Monovalent birth dose required.** Combination vaccines (Pediarix) can be used for subsequent doses.
**Minimum age for final dose:** 24 weeks (6 months).

### DTaP (Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis)

| CVX Code | Dose | Age | Minimum Interval |
|----------|------|-----|-----------------|
| 20 | Dose 1 | 2 months | -- |
| 20 | Dose 2 | 4 months | 4 weeks |
| 20 | Dose 3 | 6 months | 4 weeks |
| 20 | Dose 4 | 15-18 months | 6 months from dose 3 |
| 20 | Dose 5 | 4-6 years | 6 months from dose 4 |

**Brand names:** Daptacel, Infanrix, Pediarix (combo), Pentacel (combo), Vaxelis (combo)
**Minimum age:** 6 weeks.
**Dose 4 minimum age:** 12 months. **Dose 5 minimum age:** 4 years.
**Dose 5 not needed** if dose 4 given at >= 4 years of age.
**Adolescent booster:** Tdap at 11-12 years (CVX 115).

**Minimum age:** 6 weeks.

| CVX Code | Dose | Age | Minimum Interval |

### Meningococcal B

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----|----------|
| 162 | MenB-4C (Bexsero) | 16-23 years (preferred 16-18) | 2 doses (>= 1 month apart) |
| 163 | MenB-FHbp (Trumenba) | 16-23 years | 2 doses (6 months apart) or 3 doses (0, 1-2, 6 months) |

**Shared clinical decision making** for ages 16-23. **Risk-based (any age >= 10):** complement deficiency, eculizumab/ravulizumab, asplenia, outbreak.

### HPV (Human Papillomavirus)

| CVX Code | Dose | Age | Schedule |
|----------|------|-----|----------|
| 165 | Dose 1 | 11-12 years (can start at 9) | -- |
| 165 | Dose 2 | 6-12 months after dose 1 | If started < 15: 2-dose series |
| 165 | Dose 3 | -- | If started >= 15: 3-dose series (0, 1-2, 6 months) |

**Minimum interval (2-dose schedule):** 5 months between doses.
**Minimum interval (3-dose schedule):** 4 weeks (1 to 2), 12 weeks (2 to 3), 5 months (1 to 3).
**Catch-up:** Through age 26 if not adequately vaccinated.

### Influenza

| CVX Code | Vaccine | Age | Schedule |
|----------|---------|-----|----------|
| 158 | IIV4 (injectable) | >= 6 months | 1-2 doses annually |
| 149 | LAIV4 (FluMist) | >= 2 years | 1-2 doses annually |

**Dosing:** Children 6 months through 8 years: 2 doses (4 weeks apart) in their first season; 1 dose in subsequent seasons. >= 9 years: 1 dose annually.
**LAIV contraindications (children):** Age < 2, immunocompromised, received influenza antiviral in past 48 hours, asthma (age 2-4 with wheezing in past 12 months).

### COVID-19

Per current ACIP recommendations. Updated annually. Check CDC website for current formulation and schedule.
- 6 months - 4 years: may need 2-3 doses depending on product and vaccination history
- 5-11 years: 1-2 doses depending on vaccination history
- 12-18 years: 1 dose of updated vaccine

## Catch-Up Schedule (Children 4 Months Through 18 Years)

For children who start late or are behind schedule, the catch-up schedule provides minimum intervals and minimum ages.

### Key Catch-Up Principles

1. **Do not restart a series.** Count all valid prior doses.
2. **Use minimum intervals** between catch-up doses.
3. **Combination vaccines** can reduce the number of injections (max 2-3 injections per visit depending on age and comfort).
4. **Simultaneous administration** of all age-appropriate vaccines at a single visit is recommended.

### Minimum Ages and Intervals Summary

| Vaccine | Minimum Age Dose 1 | Min Interval 1-2 | Min Interval 2-3 | Min Interval 3-4 | Min Interval 4-5 |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| HepB | Birth | 4 weeks | 8 weeks (16 weeks from dose 1) | -- | -- |
| RV | 6 weeks | 4 weeks | 4 weeks | -- | -- |
| DTaP | 6 weeks | 4 weeks | 4 weeks | 6 months | 6 months |
| Hib | 6 weeks | 4 weeks (8 weeks if dose 1 at 12-14mo) | 4 weeks (8 weeks booster) | 8 weeks | -- |
| PCV | 6 weeks | 4 weeks (8 weeks if >= 12mo) | 4 weeks (8 weeks if >= 12mo) | 8 weeks | -- |
| IPV | 6 weeks | 4 weeks | 4 weeks (6 months for final) | 6 months | -- |
| MMR | 12 months | 4 weeks | -- | -- | -- |
| Varicella | 12 months | 3 months (< 13y) / 4 weeks (>= 13y) | -- | -- | -- |
| HepA | 12 months | 6 months | -- | -- | -- |
| MenACWY | 2 months (risk) / 11 years (routine) | 8 weeks | -- | -- | -- |
| HPV | 9 years | 5 months (2-dose) / 4 weeks (3-dose) | 12 weeks / 5 months from dose 1 | -- | -- |
| Tdap | 7 years (age 7-18 catch-up) | 4 weeks | 6 months | -- | -- |

### Age-Based Catch-Up Notes

**7-10 years (missed childhood vaccines):**
- DTaP series incomplete: Use Tdap for one dose, then Td for remaining. Or use DTaP if < 7 at series start.
- IPV: Complete series; final dose >= 4 years and >= 6 months after previous.
- MMR: 2 doses, 4 weeks apart.
- Varicella: 2 doses, 3 months apart.
- HepB: 3-dose series if not completed.

**11-18 years:**
- Tdap: 1 dose regardless of interval since last Td/DTaP (then Td every 10 years).
- MenACWY: Dose 1 at 11-12, boost at 16.
- HPV: Complete series. 2-dose if started before 15th birthday; 3-dose if started at 15+.
