# Prenatal Complications Reference

## Gestational Diabetes Screening Protocol (ACOG)

### Universal Screening Approach

All pregnant patients without pre-existing diabetes undergo screening at 24-28 weeks.

Early screening (first trimester) recommended if risk factors:
- BMI >= 25 (or >= 23 in Asian Americans)
- Prior gestational diabetes
- First-degree relative with diabetes
- High-risk race/ethnicity (African American, Hispanic, Native American, South Asian, Pacific Islander)
- History of PCOS
- Prior macrosomic infant (>= 4000g)
- HbA1c >= 5.7% or IFG or IGT on prior testing

### Two-Step Screening (ACOG Preferred)

**Step 1: 1-Hour Glucose Challenge Test (GCT)**
- 50g oral glucose load (non-fasting)
- LOINC: 1504-0
- Positive screen: >= 130 mg/dL (higher sensitivity) or >= 140 mg/dL (higher specificity)
- >= 200 mg/dL: presumptive GDM, may skip 3-hour GTT

**Step 2: 3-Hour Glucose Tolerance Test (GTT)**
- 100g oral glucose load after overnight fast (8-14 hours)
- 4 blood draws: fasting, 1hr, 2hr, 3hr

Carpenter-Coustan criteria (ACOG):

| Timepoint | LOINC | Threshold |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Fasting | 1507-3 | >= 95 mg/dL |
| 1 hour | 1518-0 | >= 180 mg/dL |
| 2 hour | 1530-5 | >= 155 mg/dL |
| 3 hour | 1547-9 | >= 140 mg/dL |

GDM diagnosis: >= 2 abnormal values.

### One-Step Screening (IADPSG/WHO Alternative)

- 75g oral glucose load, fasting
- 3 blood draws: fasting, 1hr, 2hr

| Timepoint | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Fasting | >= 92 mg/dL |
| 1 hour | >= 180 mg/dL |
| 2 hour | >= 153 mg/dL |

GDM diagnosis: >= 1 abnormal value (more inclusive criteria).

### GDM Management

**Initial management (1-2 weeks trial):**
- Medical nutrition therapy (MNT): 3 meals + 2-3 snacks, 33-40% carbohydrates
- Moderate exercise: 30 minutes most days
- Blood glucose monitoring 4x daily: fasting + 1hr or 2hr postprandial

**Glucose targets:**
- Fasting: < 95 mg/dL
- 1-hour postprandial: < 140 mg/dL
- 2-hour postprandial: < 120 mg/dL

**Pharmacotherapy (if targets not met):**
- Insulin is preferred (ACOG): start with bedtime NPH if fasting elevated, add rapid-acting if postprandial elevated
- Metformin: alternative if patient declines insulin (crosses placenta, long-term effects under study)
- Glyburide: previously common, now less recommended due to neonatal hypoglycemia risk

### GDM Fetal Surveillance
- Ultrasound for fetal growth every 4 weeks starting 28-32 weeks
- NST/BPP: start 32 weeks if on insulin, 36 weeks if diet-controlled
- Delivery timing: 39-40 weeks if well-controlled, 37-39 weeks if poorly controlled or on medication

### Postpartum GDM Follow-up
- 2-hour 75g GTT at 4-12 weeks postpartum (screen for persistent diabetes)
- Lifetime screening every 1-3 years (50% risk of type 2 DM within 10 years)
- Encourage breastfeeding, weight management, exercise

## Preeclampsia Screening and Prevention

### Definitions

| Condition | Criteria |
|-----------|---------|
| Gestational hypertension | BP >= 140/90 after 20 weeks, no proteinuria, no end-organ dysfunction |
| Preeclampsia (without severe features) | BP >= 140/90 after 20 weeks AND proteinuria (>= 300mg/24hr or P/C ratio >= 0.3) OR end-organ dysfunction |
| Preeclampsia with severe features | BP >= 160/110 OR thrombocytopenia (plt < 100k) OR liver transaminases > 2x normal OR creatinine > 1.1 mg/dL OR pulmonary edema OR cerebral/visual symptoms |
| Eclampsia | Preeclampsia + new-onset seizures |
| HELLP syndrome | Hemolysis + Elevated Liver enzymes + Low Platelets |

### Risk Factors for Preeclampsia

**High-risk factors (any one = aspirin recommended):**
- Prior preeclampsia
- Multifetal gestation
- Chronic hypertension
- Type 1 or 2 diabetes
- Renal disease
- Autoimmune disease (SLE, antiphospholipid syndrome)

**Moderate-risk factors (>= 2 = aspirin recommended):**
- Nulliparity
- BMI >= 30
- Family history of preeclampsia (mother or sister)
- Age >= 35
- Sociodemographic factors (African American)
- Prior adverse pregnancy outcome (IUGR, abruption, stillbirth)
- IVF pregnancy
- Interpregnancy interval > 10 years

### Aspirin Prophylaxis Protocol (USPTF/ACOG)

- **Indication**: High-risk factor(s) OR >= 2 moderate risk factors
- **Dose**: Low-dose aspirin 81 mg daily
- **Timing**: Start between 12-16 weeks (before 16 weeks optimal), continue until delivery
- **Evidence**: Reduces preeclampsia risk by ~24% (ASPRE trial)

### Preeclampsia Laboratory Monitoring

| Test | LOINC | Frequency | Threshold |
|------|-------|-----------|-----------|
| Platelet count | 777-3 | Each visit if suspected | < 100,000/uL (severe) |
| Creatinine | 2160-0 | Each visit if suspected | > 1.1 mg/dL (severe) |
| AST | 1920-8 | Each visit if suspected | > 2x upper limit of normal |
| ALT | 1742-6 | Each visit if suspected | > 2x upper limit of normal |
| LDH | 2532-0 | If HELLP suspected | > 600 IU/L |
| Uric acid | 3084-1 | Trending | > 6.0 mg/dL (associated with worse outcomes) |
| 24-hour urine protein | 2889-4 | Once if proteinuria detected | >= 300 mg/24hr diagnostic |
| Protein/creatinine ratio | 2890-2 | Spot urine alternative | >= 0.3 diagnostic |

### Management by Severity

**Gestational hypertension / preeclampsia without severe features:**
- Expectant management until 37 weeks
- BP monitoring 2x/week minimum
- Weekly labs: CBC, creatinine, LFTs
- Weekly fetal surveillance (NST/BPP)
- Deliver at 37 weeks

**Preeclampsia with severe features:**
- If >= 34 weeks: deliver after stabilization
- If < 34 weeks: magnesium sulfate + corticosteroids (betamethasone), attempt expectant management to 34 weeks if stable
- Antihypertensives for systolic >= 160 or diastolic >= 110: IV labetalol, IV hydralazine, or PO nifedipine
- Magnesium sulfate for seizure prophylaxis: 4-6g IV loading, 1-2g/hr maintenance

## Rh Typing and Antibody Protocols

### Rh-Negative Management

| Event | Action | Timing |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Initial labs | Rh type + antibody screen | First prenatal visit |
| Routine prophylaxis | Rhogam 300 mcg IM | 28 weeks |
| Repeat antibody screen | Confirm no sensitization | 28 weeks (before Rhogam) |
| Delivery | Newborn blood type + Coombs | At delivery |
| Rh-positive infant | Rhogam 300 mcg IM within 72 hours | Postpartum |
| First trimester loss | Rhogam 50 mcg (MICRhoGAM) | Within 72 hours |
| Second/third trimester event | Rhogam 300 mcg IM | Within 72 hours |

### Sensitizing Events Requiring Rhogam

- Spontaneous or therapeutic abortion
- Ectopic pregnancy
- Molar pregnancy
- Chorionic villus sampling
- Amniocentesis
- External cephalic version
- Abdominal trauma
- Significant vaginal bleeding (abruption, previa)
- Intrauterine fetal demise
- Delivery (vaginal or cesarean)

### Rh Alloimmunization (Positive Antibody Screen)

If anti-D antibody titer detected:
- Critical titer: >= 1:16 (some labs use 1:32)
- Below critical titer: serial titers every 4 weeks
- At or above critical titer: MCA Doppler every 1-2 weeks starting 18-20 weeks to assess for fetal anemia
- MCA PSV > 1.5 MoM: suggests fetal anemia, consider cordocentesis/transfusion
- Referral to MFM (maternal-fetal medicine) specialist

## GBS Screening Protocol

### Universal Screening

- Screen all pregnant patients at 36 weeks 0 days to 37 weeks 6 days
- Specimen: vaginal-rectal swab (lower vagina then rectum through anal sphincter)
- LOINC: 76685-3 (Group B strep culture)
- Results valid for 5 weeks

### GBS-Positive Intrapartum Prophylaxis

**Recommended regimen:**
- Penicillin G: 5 million units IV loading, then 2.5-3 million units IV every 4 hours until delivery

**Alternative regimens:**
- Ampicillin: 2g IV loading, then 1g IV every 4 hours
- PCN allergy (low anaphylaxis risk): Cefazolin 2g IV loading, then 1g IV every 8 hours
- PCN allergy (high anaphylaxis risk, GBS susceptible to clindamycin): Clindamycin 900mg IV every 8 hours
- PCN allergy (high risk, GBS resistant to clindamycin): Vancomycin 20mg/kg IV every 8 hours (max 2g per dose)

### GBS Prophylaxis Not Needed

- Planned cesarean delivery before labor onset and before ROM (regardless of GBS status)
- GBS-negative culture result within 5 weeks of delivery

## Fetal Growth Monitoring

### Fundal Height Assessment (McDonald's Rule)

- Fundal height (cm) approximately equals gestational age (weeks) from 20-36 weeks
- Measure from pubic symphysis to uterine fundus
- Discrepancy >= 3 cm from expected: order growth ultrasound

### Causes of Size Greater Than Dates

- Incorrect dating
- Multiple gestation
- Macrosomia (GDM, genetic)
- Polyhydramnios
- Uterine fibroids
- Molar pregnancy

### Causes of Size Less Than Dates

- Incorrect dating
- Intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR)
- Oligohydramnios
- Transverse lie
- Fetal demise

### IUGR Classification

| Type | Features | Etiology |
|------|----------|----------|
| Symmetric IUGR | All measurements proportionally small, HC/AC ratio normal | Early insult: genetic, chromosomal, early infection (TORCH) |
| Asymmetric IUGR | AC small relative to HC, head-sparing effect | Late insult: placental insufficiency, preeclampsia, chronic hypertension |

### IUGR Surveillance

- EFW < 10th percentile: diagnosis of small for gestational age (SGA)
- EFW < 3rd percentile: severe IUGR
- Umbilical artery Doppler: absent or reversed end-diastolic flow = critical finding
- Surveillance frequency: every 1-2 weeks with Doppler, NST/BPP
- Delivery timing: depends on Doppler findings and gestational age

## Preterm Labor Risk Factors

### Major Risk Factors

- Prior preterm birth (strongest predictor: recurrence risk 15-50%)
- Short cervix (< 25mm on transvaginal ultrasound before 24 weeks)
- Multiple gestation
- Uterine anomalies (bicornuate, septate)
- History of cervical procedures (LEEP, cone biopsy)
- Current pregnancy complications (preeclampsia, PPROM, placental abruption)

### Moderate Risk Factors

- African American race
- Low prepregnancy BMI (< 19.8)
- Cigarette smoking
- Short interpregnancy interval (< 18 months)
- Periodontal disease
- Urinary tract infections / bacterial vaginosis
- Domestic violence
- Low socioeconomic status

### Prevention Strategies

| Intervention | Indication | Evidence |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| 17-alpha hydroxyprogesterone caproate (17-OHP) | Prior spontaneous preterm birth, singleton | Weekly IM injections 16-36 weeks (note: recent PROLONG trial questions efficacy) |
| Vaginal progesterone | Short cervix (< 20mm) before 24 weeks, singleton, no prior preterm birth | 200mg vaginal suppository nightly |
| Cervical cerclage | History-indicated (prior preterm birth), ultrasound-indicated (short cervix < 25mm + prior preterm birth), or rescue (dilated cervix < 24 weeks) | Surgical |
| Cervical pessary | Short cervix, under investigation | Arabin pessary (evidence mixed) |

### Preterm Labor Management (If Active Preterm Labor < 37 Weeks)

- Tocolysis: nifedipine or indomethacin (< 32 weeks) to gain 48 hours for steroids
- Antenatal corticosteroids: betamethasone 12mg IM x 2 doses (24 hours apart) if 24-34 weeks
- Late preterm steroids: betamethasone if 34-36 weeks 6 days and anticipated delivery within 7 days
- Magnesium sulfate for neuroprotection: if < 32 weeks
- GBS prophylaxis: if GBS status unknown and delivery anticipated
