# Growth Chart Reference Data

## Chart Selection by Age

| Age Range | Chart System | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Birth to 23.9 months | WHO Growth Standards (2006) | Based on international breastfed infants as the growth standard |
| 2 to 20 years | CDC Growth Charts (2000) | Based on US population reference data |

WHO charts are standards (how children should grow under optimal conditions). CDC charts are references (how US children did grow). WHO is recommended for children under 2 by both CDC and AAP.

## Z-Score Calculation (LMS Method)

The LMS method uses three age- and sex-specific parameters:
- **L** (lambda): Box-Cox power transformation
- **M** (mu): Median
- **S** (sigma): Generalized coefficient of variation

### Formula

When L != 0:
```
Z = ((X/M)^L - 1) / (L * S)
```

When L = 0:
```
Z = ln(X/M) / S
```

Where X is the measured value.

### Clinical Significance of Z-Scores

| Z-score Range | Clinical Interpretation |
|--------------|----------------------|
| < -3 | Severely below normal -- urgent evaluation |
| -3 to -2 | Below normal -- further evaluation recommended |
| -2 to -1 | Low-normal -- monitor |
| -1 to +1 | Normal range |
| +1 to +2 | High-normal to overweight range (for BMI) |
| +2 to +3 | Above normal -- obesity range (for BMI), or tall stature |
| > +3 | Severely above normal -- evaluate for pathology |

## Preterm Infant Corrected Age

For infants born before 37 weeks, use corrected gestational age for growth chart plotting:

**Corrected age** = Chronological age - (40 weeks - Gestational age at birth)

Example: A baby born at 28 weeks who is now 6 months chronological age:
- Corrected age = 6 months - (40-28 weeks) = 6 months - 3 months = 3 months corrected
- Plot at 3 months on growth chart

**Duration of correction:**
- Weight: correct until 24 months chronological age
- Length/height: correct until 24 months
- Head circumference: correct until 18-24 months (some use up to 40 months)
