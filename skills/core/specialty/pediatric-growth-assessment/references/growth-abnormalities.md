# Growth Abnormalities Reference

## Failure to Thrive (FTT)

### Diagnostic Criteria

No single universally accepted definition. Use any of the following:
- Weight < 5th percentile for age on more than one occasion
- Weight-for-length < 5th percentile (children under 2 years)
- BMI-for-age < 5th percentile (children 2-20 years)
- Weight crossing downward through 2 or more major percentile lines over 3-6 months
- Weight < 80% of ideal body weight for age (Waterlow classification)
- Weight velocity below the 5th percentile for age over 3+ months

### FTT Classification

| Type | Mechanism | Prevalence |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Inadequate caloric intake | Feeding difficulties, psychosocial deprivation, poverty, neglect | ~70-80% of FTT |
| Inadequate caloric absorption | Celiac disease, cystic fibrosis, cow's milk protein allergy, inflammatory bowel disease | ~15-20% |
| Increased metabolic demand | Congenital heart disease, chronic lung disease, malignancy, chronic infection, hyperthyroidism | ~5-10% |

### FTT Workup

**Initial laboratory evaluation:**

| Test | LOINC | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| CBC with differential | 58410-2 | Anemia, infection, malignancy |
| CMP (or BMP) | 24323-8 | Electrolytes, renal function, hepatic function, albumin |
| ESR or CRP | 30341-2 / 1988-5 | Inflammation |
| Urinalysis + culture | 24356-8 / 630-4 | UTI, renal disease |
| Celiac panel (tTG-IgA + total IgA) | 31017-7 / 2458-8 | Celiac disease (screen all FTT patients) |
| TSH | 3016-3 | Hypothyroidism |
| Lead level | 5671-3 | Lead toxicity (if risk factors) |
| Stool studies | -- | Fat malabsorption (72-hr fecal fat), ova/parasites, occult blood |

**Second-line testing (if initial workup unrevealing):**
- Sweat chloride (cystic fibrosis)
- Immunoglobulin levels (immunodeficiency)
- Bone age X-ray (constitutional growth delay vs pathologic)
- Metabolic screen (amino acids, organic acids)
- Stool elastase (exocrine pancreatic insufficiency)

### FTT Management Approach

1. **Caloric assessment**: Calculate current intake vs estimated needs (typically 120-150% of RDA for catch-up growth)
2. **Nutritional intervention**: Caloric density enhancement (add oil, butter to foods), frequent small meals, high-calorie supplements
3. **Feeding observation**: Assess feeding dynamics, parent-child interaction
4. **Treat underlying cause** if identified
5. **Follow-up**: Weight check every 1-2 weeks initially
6. **Referral criteria**: No improvement in 3 months, severe malnutrition (weight < 60% IBW), identified organic cause requiring specialist management

### Expected Catch-Up Growth Rates

| Age | Expected Weight Gain |
|-----|---------------------|
| 0-3 months | 26-31 g/day |
| 3-6 months | 17-18 g/day |
| 6-9 months | 12-13 g/day |
| 9-12 months | 9 g/day |
| 1-3 years | 7-9 g/day |
| 4-6 years | 5-6 g/day |

Catch-up growth should exceed these normal rates to restore percentile position.

## Obesity Screening (AAP Recommendations)

### BMI Screening Schedule

- Calculate BMI at every well-child visit starting at age 2
- Plot BMI-for-age on CDC growth charts
- Document percentile at each visit

### Classification

| Category | BMI-for-Age Percentile | Action |
|----------|----------------------|--------|
| Underweight | < 5th | Evaluate for FTT or eating disorder |
| Healthy weight | 5th to < 85th | Anticipatory guidance on nutrition and activity |
| Overweight | 85th to < 95th | Assess for comorbidities, counseling, rescreen in 6 months |
| Obesity (Class I) | >= 95th to < 120% of 95th | Comprehensive evaluation, structured weight management |
| Severe obesity (Class II) | >= 120% of 95th percentile or BMI >= 35 | Referral to multidisciplinary weight management program |
| Severe obesity (Class III) | >= 140% of 95th percentile or BMI >= 40 | Referral, consider bariatric surgery evaluation if >= 13y |

### Comorbidity Screening for Overweight/Obese Children

| Comorbidity | Screening Test | When to Screen |
|------------|---------------|----------------|
| Type 2 diabetes | Fasting glucose or HbA1c or OGTT | Start at age 10 or puberty (whichever first) if BMI >= 85th + risk factor, or BMI >= 95th; repeat every 3 years |
| Dyslipidemia | Fasting lipid panel | Age 10 (or 2 years after diagnosis of obesity); non-fasting acceptable for initial screen |
| NAFLD | ALT | Annual starting at age 10 if obese; age 10 if overweight + risk factors |
| Hypertension | BP measurement | Every visit; confirm on 3 separate occasions |
| Sleep apnea | Sleep history, snoring questionnaire | Every visit; refer for polysomnography if symptomatic |
| Depression | PHQ-A or PHQ-2 | Annual screening |
| PCOS | Menstrual history, signs of hyperandrogenism | Pubertal females with obesity and irregular menses |

### Weight Management Approach (AAP Clinical Practice Guideline 2023)

**Stage 1 -- Prevention Plus (all overweight/obese):**
- >= 5 servings fruits/vegetables daily
- Minimize sugar-sweetened beverages
- Limit screen time to <= 2 hours/day
- >= 60 minutes physical activity daily
- Family meals, healthy eating environment
- Follow-up: 3-6 months; if no improvement, advance to Stage 2

**Stage 2 -- Structured Weight Management:**
- Structured meal plan with dietitian
- Supervised physical activity program
- Behavioral counseling
- Monthly follow-up
- Consider referral if no improvement in 3-6 months

**Stage 3 -- Comprehensive Multidisciplinary Intervention:**
- Pediatric weight management team (physician, dietitian, behavioral health, exercise specialist)
- Intensive behavioral therapy (>= 26 hours over 3-12 months)
- Weekly follow-up initially

**Stage 4 -- Tertiary Care:**
- Pharmacotherapy (if >= 12 years): orlistat, GLP-1 agonists (liraglutide approved >= 12, semaglutide >= 12), phentermine/topiramate (>= 12)
- Bariatric surgery consideration (if >= 13 years with severe obesity and comorbidities)

## Short Stature Workup

### Definition

Short stature: height < 3rd percentile for age and sex, or > 2 standard deviations below mean.

### When to Evaluate

- Height < 3rd percentile (z-score < -1.88)
- Growth velocity < 25th percentile for age (see velocity norms below)
- Height crossing downward through 2+ major percentile lines after age 2
- Predicted adult height significantly below mid-parental target height
- Disproportionate short stature (abnormal upper-to-lower segment ratio)

### Mid-Parental Target Height

**Boys**: (Mother's height + Father's height + 13 cm) / 2
**Girls**: (Mother's height + Father's height - 13 cm) / 2

Target range: mid-parental height +/- 8.5 cm (2 SD)

### Differential Diagnosis

| Category | Conditions | Key Features |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Constitutional growth delay | Delayed bone age, family history of late puberty | Most common cause in boys; normal growth velocity, delayed skeletal maturation, late puberty, normal adult height |
| Familial short stature | Short parents, normal bone age | Normal growth velocity, bone age = chronological age, short parents, short adult height (within target) |
| GH deficiency | Low IGF-1, failed GH stimulation | Decelerated growth velocity, increased truncal fat, hypoglycemia (infants), micropenis (boys), low IGF-1 |
| Hypothyroidism | Elevated TSH, low free T4 | Decelerated growth, weight gain, constipation, fatigue, delayed bone age |
| Turner syndrome (45,X) | Females, short stature + other features | Short stature, webbed neck, wide-spaced nipples, ovarian failure, cardiac/renal anomalies |
| Celiac disease | tTG-IgA positive | GI symptoms not always present; FTT, anemia, short stature may be only signs |
| Chronic disease | IBD, CKD, CF, JIA | Growth failure may precede diagnosis |
| Skeletal dysplasia | Disproportionate short stature | Achondroplasia (most common), hypochondroplasia; disproportionate limbs vs trunk |
| Psychosocial deprivation | Neglect, emotional abuse | Reversible with improved environment |
| Small for gestational age (SGA) without catch-up | Born SGA, no catch-up by age 2-3 | ~10-15% of SGA infants fail to catch up |

### Short Stature Laboratory Workup

| Test | LOINC | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| CBC | 58410-2 | Chronic disease, anemia |
| CMP | 24323-8 | Renal, hepatic function |
| ESR or CRP | 30341-2 / 1988-5 | Inflammatory conditions (IBD, JIA) |
| TSH + Free T4 | 3016-3 / 3024-7 | Hypothyroidism |
| IGF-1 | 2484-4 | GH axis screening (age-appropriate ranges) |
| IGFBP-3 | 53959-7 | GH axis screening (more reliable in young children) |
| Celiac panel (tTG-IgA + total IgA) | 31017-7 / 2458-8 | Celiac disease |
| Karyotype | -- | Turner syndrome (all short girls without explanation) |
| Bone age X-ray (left hand/wrist) | -- | Skeletal maturation assessment |

### Referral Criteria to Pediatric Endocrinology

- Height < 1st percentile (z < -2.33) without explanation
- Growth velocity persistently < 25th percentile for age
- Bone age delayed > 2 years from chronological age
- Low IGF-1 or IGFBP-3 for age
- Suspected Turner syndrome
- Disproportionate short stature
- Known GH deficiency or pituitary pathology
- SGA without catch-up growth by age 3-4

## Head Circumference Abnormalities

### Microcephaly

- HC < 3rd percentile (z < -2) for age and sex
- HC crossing downward through 2+ percentile lines

**Causes:**
- Congenital: genetic syndromes, TORCH infections, fetal alcohol syndrome, chromosomal abnormalities
- Acquired: hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy, meningitis, craniosynostosis (usually not microcephalic but can be), metabolic disorders

**Workup:** Brain MRI, genetic testing, TORCH titers (if congenital), metabolic screen

### Macrocephaly

- HC > 97th percentile (z > 2) for age and sex
- HC crossing upward through 2+ percentile lines (more concerning than static macrocephaly)

**Causes:**
- Benign familial macrocephaly (most common -- measure parents' HC)
- Hydrocephalus (rapidly enlarging HC, bulging fontanelle, sunsetting eyes)
- Megalencephaly (brain overgrowth -- genetic syndromes)
- Subdural collections (consider non-accidental trauma)

**Workup:** Measure parents' HC, head ultrasound (if fontanelle open), brain MRI if rapidly crossing percentiles or symptomatic
