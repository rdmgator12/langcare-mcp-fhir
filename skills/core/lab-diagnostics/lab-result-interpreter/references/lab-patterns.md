# Common Abnormal Lab Patterns with Differential Diagnoses

Pattern recognition across multiple lab values to guide clinical reasoning.

## Liver Enzyme Patterns

### Hepatocellular Pattern (ALT/AST >> ALP)
R-factor > 5 (R = [ALT/ALT_ULN] / [ALP/ALP_ULN])

| Pattern | ALT | AST | AST/ALT | ALP | Bilirubin | Other Findings | Top Differentials |
|---------|-----|-----|---------|-----|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Massive elevation (> 1000 IU/L) | > 1000 | > 1000 | Variable | Mild elevation | Variable | -- | Acute viral hepatitis (HAV, HBV), acetaminophen toxicity, ischemic hepatitis ("shock liver"), acute Budd-Chiari |
| Moderate elevation (150-1000) | 150-1000 | 150-1000 | < 1 | Mild | Variable | -- | Viral hepatitis, autoimmune hepatitis, drug-induced (statins, isoniazid, phenytoin), Wilson disease |
| Mild elevation (< 150), AST > ALT | < 150 | > ALT | > 2 | Mild | Variable | GGT elevated, macrocytosis | Alcoholic hepatitis, cirrhosis |
| Mild elevation, ALT > AST | > AST | < ALT | < 1 | Normal-mild | Normal | Metabolic syndrome | NAFLD/NASH, chronic viral hepatitis, celiac disease |

### Cholestatic Pattern (ALP >> ALT/AST)
R-factor < 2

| ALP | GGT | Bilirubin | Imaging | Top Differentials |
|-----|-----|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| Elevated | Elevated | Elevated | Dilated ducts | Choledocholithiasis, pancreatic head mass, cholangiocarcinoma |
| Elevated | Elevated | Elevated | Normal ducts | PBC (check AMA), PSC (check MRCP), drug-induced (augmentin, anabolic steroids), infiltrative disease |
| Elevated | Normal | Normal | Normal | Bone disease (Paget, fracture, metastasis), pregnancy (placental), adolescent growth |
| Elevated | Elevated | Normal | Normal | Early cholestasis, drug reaction, fatty liver |

### Mixed Pattern
R-factor 2-5: Hepatocellular + cholestatic components. Consider: drug-induced liver injury, autoimmune overlap syndromes, granulomatous hepatitis.

### Hemolytic Anemia Workup Pattern
- Elevated reticulocyte count
- Elevated LDH
- Elevated indirect bilirubin
- Low haptoglobin (consumed)
- Peripheral smear: schistocytes (microangiopathic), spherocytes (autoimmune/hereditary spherocytosis)
- Direct Coombs: positive = autoimmune; negative = non-immune (TTP/HUS, DIC, mechanical valve, PNH)

## Iron Studies Patterns

| Diagnosis | Iron | TIBC | Ferritin | Transferrin Sat | Reticulocytes |
|-----------|------|------|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Iron deficiency | Low | High | Low | < 20% | Low |
| Anemia of chronic disease | Low | Low | Normal/High | 15-20% | Low |
| Iron overload (hemochromatosis) | High | Low | Very high | > 45% | Normal |
| Sideroblastic anemia | High | Normal | High | > 45% | Low |
| Thalassemia trait | Normal/High | Normal | Normal/High | Normal | Normal/High |
| Combined iron deficiency + chronic disease | Low | Variable | Low-Normal | < 20% | Low |

Key discriminator: Ferritin < 30 ng/mL is essentially diagnostic of iron deficiency (sensitivity ~92%). Ferritin 30-100 ng/mL with transferrin sat < 20% may still represent iron deficiency (especially in inflammatory states where ferritin is elevated as acute phase reactant).

### Sick Euthyroid (Non-thyroidal Illness)
- Pattern: low T3, low/normal T4, low/normal/mildly elevated TSH
- Seen in: critical illness, starvation, post-surgical states
- Action: do not treat with thyroid hormone; recheck after acute illness resolves

## Pancytopenia Workup

All three lineages decreased: WBC low, Hemoglobin low, Platelets low.

| Pattern | Reticulocytes | Peripheral Smear | LDH/Haptoglobin | Differentials |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Low reticulocytes | Low | Normal morphology | Normal | Aplastic anemia, myelodysplastic syndrome, bone marrow infiltration (leukemia, lymphoma, myelofibrosis, metastatic cancer), B12/folate deficiency (severe), drug-induced (chemotherapy, methotrexate, TMP-SMX) |
| Low reticulocytes | Low | Blasts present | Normal | Acute leukemia (AML, ALL) |
| Low reticulocytes | Low | Leukoerythroblastic picture | Normal | Myelofibrosis, marrow infiltration |
| High reticulocytes | High | Schistocytes | High LDH, low hapto | Microangiopathic hemolytic anemia (TTP, HUS, DIC) with consumption |
| Variable | Variable | Hypersegmented neutrophils, oval macrocytes | Normal-mild elevation | Megaloblastic anemia (B12 or folate deficiency) |

## Electrolyte Patterns

### Hyponatremia (Na < 136 mEq/L) Algorithmic Approach
1. Check serum osmolality:
   - Low (< 280): true hyponatremia
   - Normal (280-295): pseudohyponatremia (hyperlipidemia, hyperproteinemia)
   - High (> 295): translocational (hyperglycemia -- correct Na: add 1.6 mEq/L per 100 mg/dL glucose above 100)
2. If true hyponatremia, check volume status:
   - Hypovolemic (low urine Na < 20): GI losses, third spacing, burns
   - Hypovolemic (high urine Na > 20): diuretics, Addison's disease, cerebral salt wasting
   - Euvolemic (urine osmolality > 100): SIADH (check medications: SSRIs, carbamazepine), hypothyroidism, adrenal insufficiency
   - Hypervolemic: CHF, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome

### Hyperkalemia (K > 5.0 mEq/L) Algorithmic Approach
1. Exclude pseudohyperkalemia: hemolyzed specimen, extreme leukocytosis, thrombocytosis
2. Check medications: ACEi/ARB, spironolactone, potassium supplements, TMP-SMX, NSAIDs, heparin
3. Check renal function: AKI, CKD (reduced excretion)
4. Check for tissue breakdown: rhabdomyolysis (CK > 5x), tumor lysis (uric acid, phosphorus elevated), massive hemolysis
5. Check for acidosis: metabolic acidosis shifts K+ extracellularly
6. Endocrine: Addison's disease (check cortisol), hypoaldosteronism

### High Anion Gap Metabolic Acidosis (MUDPILES)
Anion gap = Na - (Cl + HCO3) > 12 mEq/L

| Letter | Cause | Key Lab Finding |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| M | Methanol | Osmolar gap > 10, visual symptoms |
| U | Uremia | BUN > 60, Cr elevated |
| D | DKA | Glucose > 250, ketones positive, pH < 7.30 |
| P | Propylene glycol | Osmolar gap, lactate (L-lactate normal, D-lactate elevated) |
| I | Isoniazid, Iron | Drug levels; iron > 500 ug/dL |
| L | Lactic acidosis | Lactate > 2 mmol/L (Type A: hypoperfusion; Type B: medications, liver failure) |
| E | Ethylene glycol | Osmolar gap, calcium oxalate crystals in urine, renal failure |
| S | Salicylates | Salicylate level > 30 mg/dL, mixed respiratory alkalosis + metabolic acidosis |

## Tumor Lysis Syndrome Lab Pattern

All occurring simultaneously or rapidly:
- Uric acid > 8 mg/dL (or > 25% increase)
- Potassium > 6.0 mEq/L (or > 25% increase)
- Phosphorus > 4.5 mg/dL (or > 25% increase)
- Calcium < 7.0 mg/dL (or > 25% decrease)
- LDH markedly elevated
- Creatinine rising

Context: occurs 12-72 hours after chemotherapy initiation, especially in high-burden hematologic malignancies (Burkitt lymphoma, ALL, AML with high WBC).

## DIC (Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation) Lab Pattern

| Analyte | Finding | Direction |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Platelets | Low (consumed) | Decreasing |
| PT/INR | Prolonged | Increasing |
| aPTT | Prolonged | Increasing |
| Fibrinogen | Low (consumed) | Decreasing |
| D-dimer | Markedly elevated | Increasing |
| Peripheral smear | Schistocytes | -- |
| LDH | Elevated | -- |
| Haptoglobin | Low | -- |

ISTH DIC Score: Platelets (> 100 = 0, 50-100 = 1, < 50 = 2) + D-dimer (no increase = 0, moderate = 2, strong = 3) + Prolonged PT (< 3s = 0, 3-6s = 1, > 6s = 2) + Fibrinogen (> 1.0 g/L = 0, < 1.0 = 1). Score >= 5 = compatible with overt DIC.
