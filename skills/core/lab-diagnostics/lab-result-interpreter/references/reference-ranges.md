### Pregnancy Adjustments (CBC)
- Hemoglobin: lower limit 11.0 g/dL (1st/3rd trimester), 10.5 g/dL (2nd trimester) -- physiologic hemodilution
- WBC: upper limit 12.0-15.0 x10^3/uL (may reach 16.0 in labor)
- Platelets: lower limit 100 x10^3/uL (gestational thrombocytopenia is common)

### Neonatal Adjustments (CBC)
- Hemoglobin: 14.0-24.0 g/dL (newborn), declining to 10.0-13.0 at 2-6 months (physiologic nadir)
- WBC: 9.0-30.0 x10^3/uL (newborn)

### Calcium Correction Formula
Corrected Calcium = Measured Total Calcium + 0.8 * (4.0 - Albumin)

### Anion Gap
Anion Gap = Na - (Cl + HCO3)
- Normal: 8-12 mEq/L (without potassium) or 10-14 mEq/L (with potassium)
- Elevated (> 12): indicates unmeasured anions -- MUDPILES differential

### BUN/Creatinine Ratio
- Normal: 10-20:1
- Elevated (> 20:1): prerenal azotemia (dehydration, CHF, GI bleed), high protein intake, catabolic state
- Low (< 10:1): liver disease, malnutrition, SIADH

### AST/ALT Ratio (De Ritis Ratio)
- AST/ALT < 1: non-alcoholic hepatitis, viral hepatitis
- AST/ALT > 2: alcoholic hepatitis (classic), Wilson disease, cirrhosis
- AST/ALT ~ 1: may be seen in various liver diseases

### R-Factor (Pattern Classification)
R = (ALT / ALT_ULN) / (ALP / ALP_ULN)
- R > 5: hepatocellular pattern
- R < 2: cholestatic pattern
- R 2-5: mixed pattern

### LDL Targets by Risk
- Very high risk (ASCVD): < 70 mg/dL (consider < 55 per ESC)
- High risk (diabetes, 10-year ASCVD >= 20%): < 70 mg/dL
- Moderate risk: < 100 mg/dL
- Low risk: < 130 mg/dL

### Friedewald Equation (LDL calculated)
LDL = Total Cholesterol - HDL - (Triglycerides / 5)
Invalid when triglycerides > 400 mg/dL. Use direct LDL measurement.
