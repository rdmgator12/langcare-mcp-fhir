# ADA Standards of Care -- Glycemic Targets and Management

Based on ADA Standards of Medical Care in Diabetes 2024. Updated annually.

## Glycemic Targets by Population

### General Adult (Non-Pregnant)

| Metric | Target | LOINC | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| HbA1c | < 7.0% | 4548-4 | Reasonable for most non-pregnant adults |
| Fasting / Pre-meal glucose | 80-130 mg/dL | 1558-6 / 2345-7 | Self-monitored or CGM |
| Peak post-prandial glucose (1-2h after meal start) | < 180 mg/dL | 2345-7 | |
| Time in Range (CGM 70-180 mg/dL) | > 70% | -- | Equivalent to A1c ~7% |
| Time below range (< 70 mg/dL) | < 4% | -- | |
| Time below range (< 54 mg/dL) | < 1% | -- | |

### Individualized Targets -- More Stringent (A1c < 6.5%)

Apply if:
- Short disease duration (< 10 years)
- Type 2 DM treated with lifestyle or metformin only (low hypoglycemia risk)
- Long life expectancy
- No significant cardiovascular disease
- Patient preference and motivation

### Individualized Targets -- Less Stringent (A1c < 8.0%)

Apply if:
- History of severe hypoglycemia
- Limited life expectancy
- Advanced microvascular or macrovascular complications
- Extensive comorbid conditions
- Long-standing diabetes where A1c < 7% has been difficult despite maximal therapy
- Older adults (> 65) with cognitive impairment or functional limitations

### Pediatric Type 1 Diabetes

| Age Group | A1c Target | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| All pediatric (ADA 2024) | < 7.0% | Previous age-stratified targets removed in 2022 |
| If frequent hypoglycemia | < 7.5% | Individualize |

### Older Adults (> 65 years)

| Health Status | A1c Target | Fasting Glucose | Rationale |
|--------------|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Healthy, few comorbidities, intact cognition | < 7.0-7.5% | 80-130 mg/dL | Reasonable life expectancy, can manage complex regimen |
| Complex, multiple comorbidities, mild cognitive impairment | < 8.0% | 90-150 mg/dL | Intermediate life expectancy, hypoglycemia risk |
| Very complex, long-term care, moderate-severe cognitive impairment, limited life expectancy | < 8.5% (or avoid A1c target) | 100-180 mg/dL | Avoid hypoglycemia; minimize symptomatic hyperglycemia |

### Hospitalized Patients

| Setting | Target | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Critically ill (ICU) | 140-180 mg/dL | Insulin drip. Tighter control (110-140) has not shown benefit and increases hypoglycemia risk. |
| Non-critically ill (floor) | Pre-meal < 140, random < 180 | Basal-bolus insulin preferred over sliding scale alone. |

## Diagnostic Criteria for Diabetes

| Test | Normal | Prediabetes | Diabetes | LOINC |
|------|--------|-------------|----------|-------|
| HbA1c | < 5.7% | 5.7-6.4% | >= 6.5% | 4548-4 |
| Fasting Plasma Glucose | < 100 mg/dL | 100-125 mg/dL (IFG) | >= 126 mg/dL | 1558-6 |
| 2-hour OGTT (75g) | < 140 mg/dL | 140-199 mg/dL (IGT) | >= 200 mg/dL | 20436-2 |
| Random Plasma Glucose | -- | -- | >= 200 mg/dL with classic symptoms | 2345-7 |

Diagnosis requires two abnormal results from the same sample or two separate tests, unless unequivocal hyperglycemia with acute metabolic decompensation.

## Medication Escalation Algorithm (Type 2 DM)

### Step 1: First-Line
**Metformin** + lifestyle modifications
- Start 500mg daily, titrate to 1000mg BID over 1-2 months
- Contraindicated: eGFR < 30 mL/min/1.73m2
- Reduce dose: eGFR 30-45 mL/min/1.73m2
- Side effects: GI (diarrhea, nausea), B12 deficiency (monitor annually)
- Hold 48h before/after iodinated contrast

### Step 2: Second Agent (if A1c not at target after 3-6 months on metformin)

Choose based on patient characteristics:

| Comorbidity | Preferred Agent | Rationale |
|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| Established ASCVD | GLP-1 RA (semaglutide, liraglutide, dulaglutide) | Proven CV mortality reduction |
| Heart failure (HFrEF) | SGLT2 inhibitor (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) | Proven HF hospitalization reduction |
| CKD (eGFR 20-60 or UACR > 200) | SGLT2 inhibitor | Proven nephroprotection (DAPA-CKD, CREDENCE, EMPA-KIDNEY) |
| Need to minimize weight gain | GLP-1 RA > SGLT2i > DPP-4i | Weight loss: GLP-1 RA > SGLT2i; weight neutral: DPP-4i |
| Need to minimize hypoglycemia | GLP-1 RA, SGLT2i, DPP-4i, TZD | Avoid sulfonylureas and meglitinides |
| Cost is primary concern | Sulfonylurea (glipizide, glimepiride) or TZD (pioglitazone) | Generic, inexpensive. Hypoglycemia risk (SU), fluid retention (TZD). |

### Step 3: Triple Therapy or Insulin
If A1c not at target after 3-6 months on dual therapy:
- Add third oral/injectable agent (different class from step 2)
- OR add basal insulin (if A1c > 9% or symptomatic hyperglycemia)

### Step 4: Insulin Intensification
- **Basal insulin**: start 10 units/day or 0.1-0.2 U/kg/day. Titrate by 2 units every 3 days to fasting glucose target.
- **Basal + 1 prandial**: add rapid-acting insulin at largest meal (start 4 units or 10% of basal dose)
- **Basal-bolus**: add prandial insulin at all meals
- **Premixed insulin**: alternative to basal-bolus for patients who prefer fewer injections

## Hypoglycemia Classification (ADA)

| Level | Glucose | Symptoms | Action |
|-------|---------|----------|--------|
| Level 1 (alert) | < 70 mg/dL | May or may not be symptomatic | 15-20g fast-acting carbs. Recheck in 15 min. Adjust regimen. |
| Level 2 (clinically significant) | < 54 mg/dL | Neuroglycopenic symptoms likely | Same as Level 1. Indicates regimen needs significant adjustment. |
| Level 3 (severe) | Any level | Altered mental status, requiring external assistance | Glucagon (nasal, IM, or IV). D50 IV if access available. Emergency medical attention. Major regimen adjustment. |

## DKA and HHS Diagnostic Criteria

### Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

| Parameter | Mild | Moderate | Severe |
|-----------|------|----------|--------|
| Plasma glucose | > 250 mg/dL | > 250 mg/dL | > 250 mg/dL |
| Arterial pH | 7.25-7.30 | 7.00-7.24 | < 7.00 |
| Serum bicarbonate | 15-18 mEq/L | 10-14.9 mEq/L | < 10 mEq/L |
| Urine ketones | Positive | Positive | Positive |
| Serum ketones | Positive | Positive | Positive |
| Anion gap | > 10 | > 12 | > 12 |
| Mental status | Alert | Alert/drowsy | Stupor/coma |

Note: Euglycemic DKA (glucose < 250) can occur with SGLT2 inhibitors. Check ketones even if glucose is not severely elevated.

### Hyperglycemic Hyperosmolar State (HHS)

| Parameter | HHS |
|-----------|-----|
| Plasma glucose | > 600 mg/dL |
| Serum osmolality | > 320 mOsm/kg |
| Arterial pH | > 7.30 |
| Serum bicarbonate | > 18 mEq/L |
| Ketones | Small or absent |
| Mental status | Often altered (stupor, coma) |
