# Preoperative Lab Requirements

Requirements per ACC/AHA perioperative guidelines, ASA Practice Advisory, and institutional consensus. Requirements are additive -- higher-risk patients need all lower-risk labs plus additional tests.

## ASA Physical Status Classification

| ASA Class | Definition | Examples | Perioperative Mortality |
|-----------|-----------|----------|------------------------|
| I | Normal healthy patient | No medical problems, non-smoker, minimal alcohol | 0.06-0.08% |
| II | Mild systemic disease | Controlled HTN, controlled DM (A1c < 8%), BMI 30-40, current smoker, social alcohol, pregnancy | 0.27-0.4% |
| III | Severe systemic disease | Poorly controlled DM (A1c > 8%), COPD on home O2, BMI > 40, active hepatitis, ESRD on dialysis, stable angina, CHF (compensated), prior MI/CVA > 3 months | 1.8-4.3% |
| IV | Severe systemic disease with constant threat to life | Recent MI/CVA < 3 months, ongoing ischemia, severe valve disease, severe CHF (EF < 25%), sepsis, DIC, ARDS | 7.8-23% |
| V | Moribund, not expected to survive without surgery | Ruptured AAA, massive trauma, intracranial hemorrhage with mass effect, bowel ischemia with sepsis | 9.4-51% |
| VI | Brain-dead organ donor | -- | -- |

## Surgical Risk Classification

### Low-Risk Surgery (Cardiac Risk < 1%)
- Cataract surgery
- Minor dermatologic procedures (excision, biopsy)
- Breast biopsy / breast lumpectomy
- Endoscopy without biopsy
- Superficial procedures
- Ambulatory surgery (most)

### Medium-Risk Surgery (Cardiac Risk 1-5%)
- Laparoscopic cholecystectomy
- Laparoscopic appendectomy
- Arthroscopic surgery (knee, shoulder)
- Hernia repair (open)
- Hysterectomy (laparoscopic or vaginal)
- Spine surgery (1-2 levels, posterior approach)
- ENT procedures (tonsillectomy, septoplasty, FESS)
- Thyroidectomy
- Urologic procedures (TURP, cystoscopy with biopsy)
- Orthopedic (carpal tunnel, rotator cuff repair)

### High-Risk Surgery (Cardiac Risk > 5%)
- Cardiac surgery (CABG, valve repair/replacement)
- Major vascular (aortic, lower extremity revascularization)
- Major orthopedic (total hip/knee replacement, spine fusion > 2 levels)
- Open abdominal surgery (colectomy, Whipple, hepatic resection)
- Thoracic surgery (lobectomy, pneumonectomy)
- Intracranial surgery (craniotomy)
- Organ transplant
- Major head and neck surgery (neck dissection, mandibular reconstruction)
- Major urologic (radical prostatectomy, cystectomy, nephrectomy)
- Emergency surgery (all)

### Condition-Specific Additional Requirements

| Condition (SNOMED) | Additional Labs | Acceptable Age | Rationale |
|--------------------|----------------|----------------|-----------|
| Diabetes (44054006, 73211009) | HbA1c | 90 days | Glycemic control assessment. Surgery generally proceed if A1c < 9%. |
| Diabetes | BMP (glucose, K+, Cr) | 30 days | Glucose management, renal function |
| CKD (709044004) | CMP, Mg, Phos | 14 days | Electrolyte management, anemia assessment |
| CKD Stage 4-5 | CBC, coag panel | 7 days | Anemia, uremic platelet dysfunction |
| Liver disease (235856003) | Liver panel, coag, albumin | 14 days | Synthetic function, coagulopathy |
| Liver cirrhosis (19943007) | MELD score components (Cr, bilirubin, INR), albumin, CBC | 7 days | MELD predicts perioperative mortality |
| CHF (42343007) | BNP or NT-proBNP | 30 days | Decompensation risk assessment |
| CHF | BMP | 14 days | Diuretic effects on electrolytes |
| Coronary artery disease (53741008) | Troponin (baseline), ECG | 24 hours pre-op / day of surgery | Baseline for postoperative comparison |
| Atrial fibrillation (49436004) on anticoag | PT/INR or Anti-Xa | 24-48 hours | Therapeutic range verification |
| Anemia (271737000) | CBC, iron studies, reticulocyte count | 14 days | Preoperative optimization (target Hgb > 10 for elective surgery) |
| Thyroid disease (14304000) | TSH | 90 days | Avoid surgery in severely hypo/hyperthyroid state |
| Adrenal insufficiency (386584007) | AM cortisol (or not required if on replacement) | 30 days | Stress dose steroids needed perioperatively |
| Obstructive sleep apnea (78275009) | ABG or SpO2 | 30 days | Baseline for post-anesthesia monitoring |
| Sickle cell disease (417357006) | CBC, reticulocyte count, HbS level | 7 days | Target HbS < 30% for high-risk surgery (may need exchange transfusion) |
| Pheochromocytoma (302835009) | Plasma metanephrines, catecholamines | 14 days | Must be alpha-blocked before surgery |
| Morbid obesity (238136002) BMI > 40 | BMP, CBC, CXR, ECG | 30 days | Increased cardiac, pulmonary, metabolic risk |

### Age-Specific Requirements

| Age Group | Additional Requirements |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Female, reproductive age (12-55) | Pregnancy test (urine HCG or serum beta-HCG) unless documented hysterectomy, bilateral oophorectomy, or post-menopausal status |
| Age > 50 | ECG recommended for medium+ risk surgery |
| Age > 65 | CBC, BMP required for all except lowest-risk procedures. ECG recommended. |
| Age > 75 | CBC, BMP/CMP, ECG required. Consider cognitive baseline assessment. |

## Surgery-Specific Lab Requirements

### Cardiac Surgery (CABG, Valve)
- CBC, CMP, PT/INR, aPTT, fibrinogen
- Type & Crossmatch (4 units pRBC, 4 FFP, 1 platelet)
- HbA1c if diabetic
- BNP or NT-proBNP
- Troponin baseline
- Pulmonary function tests if thoracotomy approach

### Total Joint Replacement (Hip/Knee)
- CBC (Hgb > 10 preferred for elective)
- BMP
- PT/INR if on anticoagulant
- Type & Screen
- HbA1c if diabetic (target < 8% per AAHKS)
- UA with culture if prosthetic joint (rule out UTI as infection source)
- ESR, CRP if revision surgery (rule out periprosthetic infection)

### Spine Surgery (Multi-level Fusion)
- CBC
- BMP/CMP
- Type & Crossmatch (2-4 units for multi-level)
- Coagulation panel
- HbA1c if diabetic (target < 8%)

### Bariatric Surgery
- CBC, CMP
- Iron studies, B12, folate, thiamine, vitamin D
- HbA1c
- Lipid panel
- TSH
- Type & Screen
- H. pylori testing (if upper GI procedure)

### Transplant Surgery (Kidney, Liver, Heart)
- Per transplant center protocol (extensive panel)
- CBC, CMP, coag, type & crossmatch, viral serologies (CMV, EBV, HIV, HBV, HCV)
- Crossmatch with donor
- Panel reactive antibody (PRA)

## Criteria for Surgery Postponement

Consider postponing elective surgery if:
- HbA1c > 9% (relative; some centers use 8%)
- Active infection at surgical site
- Uncontrolled hypertension (SBP > 180 or DBP > 110 at time of surgery)
- Acute coronary syndrome within 60 days
- Decompensated heart failure
- Severe anemia (Hgb < 8) without transfusion plan
- INR > 1.5 (unless corrected/correctable before surgery)
- Platelet count < 50,000 (unless transfusion plan in place)
- Active DKA or HHS
- Uncorrected severe electrolyte abnormality (K+ < 3.0 or > 6.0, Na+ < 125 or > 155)
- Uncorrected severe coagulopathy
