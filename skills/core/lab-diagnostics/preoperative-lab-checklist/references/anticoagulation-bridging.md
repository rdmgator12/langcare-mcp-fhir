# Anticoagulation Bridging Protocols

Perioperative management of anticoagulant and antiplatelet agents. Based on ACC/AHA, ACCP, and ASH guidelines.

## Procedure Bleeding Risk Classification

### Low Bleeding Risk
- Cataract surgery
- Minor dermatologic procedures
- Dental procedures (extraction, cleaning, root canal)
- Endoscopy without biopsy
- Pacemaker/ICD implantation
- Minor orthopedic (carpal tunnel)
- Arthrocentesis

### Moderate Bleeding Risk
- Abdominal surgery (laparoscopic)
- Hernia repair
- Arthroscopy
- Non-cardiac thoracic surgery
- Endoscopy with biopsy
- Prostate biopsy

### High Bleeding Risk
- Cardiac surgery
- Intracranial / spinal surgery
- Major orthopedic (joint replacement, spine fusion)
- Major vascular surgery
- Major abdominal surgery (open)
- Liver / kidney biopsy
- Polypectomy (large polyps)
- Epidural / spinal anesthesia placement
- Transurethral prostate resection
- Kidney surgery

## Warfarin Perioperative Management

### Thromboembolic Risk Stratification

**High Risk (annual VTE/stroke risk > 10%):**
- Mechanical mitral valve (any)
- Mechanical aortic valve (older generation: caged ball, tilting disc)
- Recent VTE (< 3 months)
- Severe thrombophilia (Protein C/S deficiency, antiphospholipid syndrome, multiple thrombophilias)
- CHA2DS2-VASc >= 7 (atrial fibrillation)
- Recent stroke/TIA (< 3 months)

**Moderate Risk (annual risk 5-10%):**
- Mechanical aortic valve (bileaflet) with additional risk factors (AF, prior stroke, CHF, age > 75, DM, HTN)
- VTE 3-12 months ago
- Non-severe thrombophilia (Factor V Leiden heterozygous, prothrombin gene mutation)
- Active cancer (treated within 6 months or palliative)
- CHA2DS2-VASc 5-6

**Low Risk (annual risk < 5%):**
- Mechanical bileaflet aortic valve without additional risk factors
- VTE > 12 months ago, no other risk factors
- Atrial fibrillation with CHA2DS2-VASc 1-4

### Warfarin Protocol

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day -5 | Stop warfarin |
| Day -3 | Start bridging LMWH (enoxaparin) if HIGH thromboembolic risk. Check INR. |
| Day -2 | Continue LMWH. |
| Day -1 | Last dose of LMWH: therapeutic dose at least 24h before surgery; prophylactic dose at least 12h before. Check INR. |
| Day 0 (surgery) | Confirm INR < 1.5 (< 1.3 for neuraxial anesthesia). If INR > 1.5: vitamin K 1-2mg PO or hold surgery. |
| Day +0/+1 (post-op) | Resume warfarin evening of surgery or POD1 (if hemostasis adequate). |
| Day +1/+2 (post-op) | Resume LMWH bridging 24h after low-risk surgery, 48-72h after high-risk surgery (when hemostasis confirmed). |
| Until INR therapeutic | Continue bridging LMWH until INR >= 2.0 for 2 consecutive days. |

**Moderate thromboembolic risk:** Bridging decision is individualized. Consider patient factors, procedure bleeding risk, prior VTE history. BRIDGE trial showed no bridging non-inferior for AF patients -- trend toward less bridging.

**Low thromboembolic risk:** No bridging. Stop warfarin day -5, resume post-op.

### CHA2DS2-VASc Score (for AF bridging decision)

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| CHF / LV dysfunction | 1 |
| Hypertension | 1 |
| Age >= 75 | 2 |
| Diabetes | 1 |
| Stroke/TIA/thromboembolism | 2 |
| Vascular disease (prior MI, PAD, aortic plaque) | 1 |
| Age 65-74 | 1 |
| Sex category (female) | 1 |

## Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs) Perioperative Management

DOACs do not require bridging therapy (short half-life, rapid onset/offset).

### DOAC Hold Times

| Drug | Half-life | Low Bleed Risk | High Bleed Risk | CrCl 30-50 mL/min | CrCl 15-30 mL/min |
|------|-----------|----------------|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Apixaban (Eliquis) | 12h | Hold 24h | Hold 48h | Hold 48h | Hold 72h |
| Rivaroxaban (Xarelto) | 5-9h (young), 11-13h (elderly) | Hold 24h | Hold 48h | Hold 48h | Hold 72h |
| Edoxaban (Savaysa) | 10-14h | Hold 24h | Hold 48h | Hold 48h | Hold 72h |
| Dabigatran (Pradaxa) | 12-17h | Hold 24-48h | Hold 48-72h | Hold 72-96h | Hold 96-120h |

### DOAC Resumption

| Procedure Risk | Resume Timing |
|----------------|---------------|
| Low bleeding risk | 24 hours post-op |
| Moderate bleeding risk | 48 hours post-op |
| High bleeding risk | 48-72 hours post-op (when surgical hemostasis confirmed) |
| Neuraxial anesthesia | Catheter removal: wait 24h (apixaban/rivaroxaban), 48h (dabigatran). Resume 6-8h after catheter removal. |

### Emergency DOAC Reversal

| Drug | Reversal Agent | Dose | Notes |
|------|---------------|------|-------|
| Dabigatran | Idarucizumab (Praxbind) | 5g IV (2 x 2.5g vials) | Specific reversal. Full reversal within minutes. |
| Apixaban, Rivaroxaban, Edoxaban | Andexanet alfa (Andexxa) | Low dose: 400mg bolus + 480mg infusion (4mg/min x 120 min). High dose: 800mg bolus + 960mg infusion. | Use high dose for rivaroxaban > 10mg or apixaban > 5mg taken within 8h. |
| Any DOAC (if specific reversal unavailable) | 4-factor PCC (Kcentra) | 50 IU/kg | Non-specific. Partially restores coagulation. |
| Dabigatran (if idarucizumab unavailable) | Hemodialysis | 4-6 hour session | Dabigatran is dialyzable (~60% removed). Other DOACs are not significantly dialyzable. |

## Antiplatelet Agent Perioperative Management

### Aspirin

| Clinical Scenario | Recommendation |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Primary prevention (no cardiac stent, no CAD) | Hold 7-10 days pre-op |
| Secondary prevention (established CAD, no recent stent) | Continue perioperatively for most surgeries. Hold only for intracranial/intraspinal surgery. |
| Recent PCI with drug-eluting stent (< 6 months) | DO NOT hold. Delay elective surgery if possible. If surgery cannot be delayed, continue aspirin and consult cardiology. |
| Bare metal stent (< 1 month) | DO NOT hold. Delay surgery. Stent thrombosis risk very high. |
| Drug-eluting stent (> 6 months) | Continue aspirin. May hold P2Y12 inhibitor (see below). |

### P2Y12 Inhibitors

| Drug | Hold Time Pre-Op | Notes |
|------|------------------|-------|
| Clopidogrel (Plavix) | 5-7 days | Standard for most situations |
| Ticagrelor (Brilinta) | 5 days | Reversible binding (shorter effective hold) |
| Prasugrel (Effient) | 7-10 days | Longest offset; most potent |
| Cangrelor (IV) | 1 hour | Ultra-short acting; used as bridge in some centers |

### Dual Antiplatelet Therapy (DAPT) -- Minimum Duration Before Elective Surgery

| Stent Type | Minimum DAPT Duration | Notes |
|------------|----------------------|-------|
| Bare metal stent (BMS) | 1 month | After 1 month, hold P2Y12 agent; continue aspirin |
| Drug-eluting stent (DES) -- newer generation | 3-6 months | ACC/AHA 2016: 6 months minimum; newer data support 3 months in low-risk patients |
| Drug-eluting stent (DES) -- older generation | 12 months | Higher late stent thrombosis risk |
| Recent ACS (with or without PCI) | 12 months | Higher ischemic risk in first year |

**If surgery is urgent and within minimum DAPT window:**
- Continue aspirin at minimum
- Consult interventional cardiology for risk/benefit assessment
- Consider cangrelor bridge (IV, ultra-short acting) in high-risk patients
- Have cath lab on standby

### Antiplatelet Resumption Post-Op

| Agent | Resume Timing |
|-------|---------------|
| Aspirin | 24 hours post-op (if was continued: no interruption) |
| Clopidogrel | 24-72 hours post-op (loading dose 300-600mg if held > 5 days) |
| Ticagrelor | 24-48 hours post-op (loading dose 180mg if held > 3 days) |
| Prasugrel | 24-72 hours post-op (loading dose 60mg if held > 7 days) |

## Special Populations

### Neuraxial Anesthesia (Epidural/Spinal)

Strict hold times to prevent epidural hematoma (catastrophic complication):

| Agent | Hold Before Placement | Hold Before Catheter Removal | Resume After Removal |
|-------|----------------------|-----------------------------|--------------------|
| Warfarin | INR < 1.4 | INR < 1.4 | Immediately after removal |
| Enoxaparin (prophylactic) | 12 hours | 12 hours | 4 hours after removal |
| Enoxaparin (therapeutic) | 24 hours | 24 hours | 4 hours after removal |
| UFH (SQ prophylactic) | 4-6 hours | 4-6 hours | 1 hour after removal |
| UFH (IV therapeutic) | 4-6 hours, check aPTT | 4-6 hours, check aPTT | 1 hour after removal |
| Apixaban | 48 hours | 48 hours | 6 hours after removal |
| Rivaroxaban | 48 hours | 48 hours | 6 hours after removal |
| Dabigatran | 72-96 hours | 72-96 hours | 6 hours after removal |
| Clopidogrel | 7 days | 7 days | Immediately after removal |
| Ticagrelor | 5 days | 5 days | 6 hours after removal |
| Prasugrel | 7-10 days | 7-10 days | 6 hours after removal |
| Aspirin | Continue (no hold needed) | Continue | Continue |

### Renal Impairment Adjustments

| Drug | CrCl > 50 | CrCl 30-50 | CrCl 15-30 | CrCl < 15 |
|------|-----------|------------|------------|-----------|
| Enoxaparin (therapeutic) | 1 mg/kg BID | 1 mg/kg BID (monitor Anti-Xa) | 1 mg/kg DAILY | Use UFH instead |
| Dabigatran | Standard dosing | Extended hold time (see above) | Not recommended | Contraindicated |
| Apixaban | Standard dosing | Standard dosing | 2.5mg BID | Limited data; use with caution |
| Rivaroxaban | Standard dosing | 15mg daily (AF) | Not recommended | Contraindicated |

### Liver Disease Adjustments

- Warfarin: increased sensitivity (reduced factor synthesis). Expect lower doses.
- DOACs: avoid in Child-Pugh B/C (apixaban may be used in Child-Pugh A/B with caution; dabigatran avoid in Child-Pugh B/C).
- Coagulopathy of liver disease is NOT protective against thrombosis -- balanced hemostatic defect.
- Viscoelastic testing (TEG/ROTEM) preferred over PT/INR for surgical hemostasis assessment in cirrhosis.
