# Complete Critical Value Thresholds Table

Comprehensive critical value thresholds with clinical actions per CAP (College of American Pathologists) and CLIA guidelines. Includes adult and pediatric thresholds, therapeutic drug monitoring, and institutional notification requirements.

## Adult Chemistry Critical Values

### Electrolytes

| Analyte | LOINC | Critical Low | Critical High | Unit | Clinical Action (Low) | Clinical Action (High) |
|---------|-------|-------------|---------------|------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Potassium | 2823-3 | < 2.5 | > 6.5 | mEq/L | Verify specimen. ECG stat. IV KCl replacement (10-20 mEq/hr via central line if < 2.0). Continuous telemetry. | Verify specimen (hemolysis?). ECG stat. Calcium gluconate if ECG changes. Insulin 10U + D50. Kayexalate or sodium zirconium cyclosilicate. Emergent dialysis if refractory. |
| Sodium | 2951-2 | < 120 | > 160 | mEq/L | Assess volume status. Serum/urine osmolality. Restrict free water (SIADH) or give normal saline (hypovolemic). Correct no faster than 8 mEq/L per 24h to prevent osmotic demyelination. | Assess volume status. Free water replacement. Correct no faster than 10-12 mEq/L per 24h to prevent cerebral edema. D5W IV. Calculate free water deficit. |
| Calcium (total) | 17861-6 | < 6.0 | > 13.0 | mg/dL | Correct for albumin. IV calcium gluconate 1-2g over 10 min. Check Mg (correct if low). ECG. Check PTH, vitamin D. | Aggressive IV NS hydration (200-300 mL/hr). Furosemide after volume repletion. Calcitonin 4 IU/kg. Zoledronic acid if malignancy. Check PTH. Dialysis if refractory. |
| Ionized Calcium | 1994-3 | < 0.78 | > 1.58 | mmol/L | IV calcium replacement. Monitor QTc (prolongation risk). | Same as total calcium high. |
| Magnesium | 19123-9 | < 1.0 | > 4.0 | mg/dL | IV magnesium sulfate 2g over 1 hour. ECG (Torsades risk). Check potassium (refractory hypokalemia if Mg low). | Stop Mg supplements. IV calcium gluconate if symptomatic. Dialysis if renal failure. |
| Phosphorus | 2777-1 | < 1.0 | > 8.0 | mg/dL | IV sodium/potassium phosphate (0.08-0.16 mmol/kg over 6h). Monitor calcium (inverse relationship). Hold phosphate binders. | Aggressive hydration. Phosphate binders (sevelamer, calcium acetate). Dialysis if renal failure. Check for tumor lysis syndrome. |

## Coagulation Critical Values

| Analyte | LOINC | Critical Threshold | Unit | Clinical Action |
|---------|-------|--------------------|------|----------------|
| PT | 5902-2 | > 30 seconds | seconds | Assess for bleeding. Check INR. Vitamin K 10mg IV if not on therapeutic anticoagulation. FFP or 4-factor PCC if active bleeding. |
| INR | 6301-6 | > 5.0 | ratio | See INR management ladder below. |
| aPTT | 3173-2 | > 100 seconds | seconds | If on heparin: hold infusion, recheck in 2-4h. If not on heparin: check mixing study (inhibitor vs factor deficiency). Assess for bleeding. |

### INR Management Ladder (on warfarin)
| INR Range | Bleeding | Action |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| 5.0-9.0 | No | Hold warfarin 1-2 doses. Vitamin K 1-2.5mg PO if high bleed risk. Recheck in 24-48h. |
| 5.0-9.0 | Minor | Hold warfarin. Vitamin K 2.5mg PO. Local measures for bleeding. Recheck in 24h. |
| > 9.0 | No | Hold warfarin. Vitamin K 2.5-5.0mg PO. Recheck in 24h. May need repeat Vitamin K. |
| Any | Major (non-life-threatening) | Hold warfarin. Vitamin K 10mg IV (slow infusion over 30 min). 4-factor PCC 25 IU/kg. FFP if PCC unavailable. |
| Any | Life-threatening | Hold warfarin. Vitamin K 10mg IV. 4-factor PCC 50 IU/kg. Recheck INR in 30 min. Consider rFVIIa if refractory. |
