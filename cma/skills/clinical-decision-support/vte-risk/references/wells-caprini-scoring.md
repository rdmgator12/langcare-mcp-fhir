# VTE Risk Scoring Reference

## Wells Criteria for DVT

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Active cancer (treatment within 6 months or palliative) | +1 |
| Paralysis, paresis, or recent plaster immobilization of lower extremity | +1 |
| Recently bedridden >3 days or major surgery within 12 weeks | +1 |
| Localized tenderness along deep venous system | +1 |
| Entire leg swollen | +1 |
| Calf swelling >3 cm compared to asymptomatic leg | +1 |
| Pitting edema confined to symptomatic leg | +1 |
| Collateral superficial veins (non-varicose) | +1 |
| Previously documented DVT | +1 |
| Alternative diagnosis as likely or more likely than DVT | -2 |

| Score | Probability | Action |
|-------|------------|--------|
| <= 0 | Low (5%) | D-dimer; if negative, DVT excluded |
| 1-2 | Moderate (17%) | D-dimer; if positive, ultrasound |
| >= 3 | High (53%) | Ultrasound (D-dimer not sufficient to exclude) |

## Wells Criteria for PE

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Clinical signs/symptoms of DVT | +3 |
| PE is #1 diagnosis or equally likely | +3 |
| Heart rate > 100 bpm | +1.5 |
| Immobilization >= 3 days or surgery in prior 4 weeks | +1.5 |
| Previous PE or DVT | +1.5 |
| Hemoptysis | +1 |
| Malignancy (treatment within 6 months or palliative) | +1 |

| Score | Probability | Action |
|-------|------------|--------|
| <= 4 | PE unlikely | D-dimer; if negative, PE excluded |
| > 4 | PE likely | CT pulmonary angiography |

## Caprini Score (Surgical VTE Risk)

### 1 Point Each
- Age 41-60
- Minor surgery planned
- History of major surgery (<1 month)
- Varicose veins
- History of inflammatory bowel disease
- Swollen legs (current)
- Obesity (BMI >25)
- Acute MI
- CHF (<1 month)
- Sepsis (<1 month)
- Serious lung disease including pneumonia (<1 month)
- Abnormal pulmonary function
- Medical patient currently on bed rest
- Oral contraceptives or HRT
- Pregnancy or postpartum (<1 month)
- History of unexplained stillbirth, recurrent spontaneous abortion, premature birth with toxemia or growth-restricted infant
- Central venous access

### 2 Points Each
- Age 61-74
- Arthroscopic surgery
- Malignancy (present or previous)
- Major surgery (>45 minutes)
- Laparoscopic surgery (>45 minutes)
- Patient confined to bed (>72 hours)
- Immobilizing plaster cast (<1 month)

### 3 Points Each
- Age >= 75
- History of DVT/PE
- Family history of thrombosis
- Factor V Leiden positive
- Prothrombin 20210A positive
- Lupus anticoagulant positive
- Anticardiolipin antibodies elevated
- Elevated serum homocysteine
- Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia
- Other congenital or acquired thrombophilia

### 5 Points Each
- Stroke (<1 month)
- Multiple trauma (<1 month)
- Acute spinal cord injury (<1 month)
- Major joint arthroplasty

### Caprini Score Interpretation and Prophylaxis

| Score | Risk Level | Incidence Without Prophylaxis | Recommended Prophylaxis |
|-------|-----------|------------------------------|------------------------|
| 0-1 | Very Low | <0.5% | Early ambulation only |
| 2 | Low | ~1.5% | Pneumatic compression devices (IPC) |
| 3-4 | Moderate | ~3.0% | LMWH or UFH + IPC |
| 5-8 | High | ~6.0% | LMWH or UFH + IPC |
| >= 9 | Very High | ~11% (up to 80% without prophylaxis in highest risk) | LMWH or UFH + IPC; consider extended prophylaxis post-discharge |

## Padua Prediction Score (Medical Patients)

| Risk Factor | Points |
|------------|--------|
| Active cancer | +3 |
| Previous VTE (excluding superficial) | +3 |
| Reduced mobility (>= 3 days bed rest) | +3 |
| Already known thrombophilic condition | +3 |
| Recent (<= 1 month) trauma and/or surgery | +2 |
| Age >= 70 | +1 |
| Heart and/or respiratory failure | +1 |
| Acute MI or ischemic stroke | +1 |
| Acute infection and/or rheumatologic disorder | +1 |
| Obesity (BMI >= 30) | +1 |
| Ongoing hormonal treatment | +1 |

| Score | Risk | Prophylaxis |
|-------|------|-------------|
| < 4 | Low (0.3% VTE rate) | No pharmacologic prophylaxis; early ambulation |
| >= 4 | High (11% VTE rate) | Pharmacologic prophylaxis (LMWH or UFH) unless contraindicated |

## Prophylaxis Options

| Agent | Dose | Duration |
|-------|------|----------|
| Enoxaparin | 40mg SC daily (medical); 30mg SC q12h (surgical/high risk) | Duration of hospitalization or per protocol |
| Heparin (UFH) | 5000 units SC q8-12h | Duration of hospitalization |
| Fondaparinux | 2.5mg SC daily | Alternative for HIT |
| Rivaroxaban | 10mg PO daily | Extended prophylaxis post-joint replacement |
| IPC (mechanical) | Bilateral lower extremity | Whenever in bed; for patients with bleeding contraindication |

## Contraindications to Pharmacologic Prophylaxis
- Active major bleeding
- Severe thrombocytopenia (platelets <25,000)
- Active HIT
- Intracranial hemorrhage (acute)
- Planned neuraxial anesthesia/lumbar puncture (hold timing per agent)
