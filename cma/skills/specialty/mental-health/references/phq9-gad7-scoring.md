# PHQ-9, GAD-7, and Mental Health Screening Scoring Reference

## PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9) -- Depression

### Questions (scored 0-3 each: Not at all / Several days / More than half the days / Nearly every day)

1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things
2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless
3. Trouble falling/staying asleep, or sleeping too much
4. Feeling tired or having little energy
5. Poor appetite or overeating
6. Feeling bad about yourself or that you are a failure
7. Trouble concentrating on things
8. Moving or speaking so slowly others noticed, or being fidgety/restless
9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead or of hurting yourself

LOINC for total: 44249-1 (PHQ-9 total score)

### Severity Classification

| Score | Severity | Clinical Action |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| 0-4 | Minimal/None | No treatment needed; rescreen per schedule |
| 5-9 | Mild | Watchful waiting; rescreen in 2-4 weeks; consider counseling |
| 10-14 | Moderate | Treatment recommended: counseling and/or medication |
| 15-19 | Moderately Severe | Active treatment: medication + counseling; consider psychiatry referral |
| 20-27 | Severe | Immediate treatment: medication + intensive therapy; psychiatry referral; assess safety |

### PHQ-9 Item 9 Safety Protocol
If Item 9 > 0 (any positive response to suicidal ideation):
1. Perform immediate safety assessment
2. Determine passive vs active ideation
3. Assess for plan, intent, means, and timeline
4. Apply Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale
5. Document safety plan
6. Consider psychiatric emergency evaluation if imminent risk

### Treatment Response Definitions
| Term | Criterion |
|------|-----------|
| Response | >= 50% reduction from baseline PHQ-9 |
| Remission | PHQ-9 score < 5 |
| Partial response | 25-49% reduction |
| Non-response | < 25% reduction after adequate trial (4-8 weeks at therapeutic dose) |

## PHQ-2 (Brief Screening)

Questions 1 and 2 from PHQ-9 only. LOINC: 55758-7

| Score | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 0-2 | Negative screen; no further assessment unless clinical suspicion |
| >= 3 | Positive screen; administer full PHQ-9 |

Sensitivity: ~83%. Specificity: ~92%.

## GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7) -- Anxiety

### Questions (scored 0-3 each)

1. Feeling nervous, anxious, or on edge
2. Not being able to stop or control worrying
3. Worrying too much about different things
4. Trouble relaxing
5. Being so restless that it is hard to sit still
6. Becoming easily annoyed or irritable
7. Feeling afraid as if something awful might happen

LOINC for total: 70274-6 (GAD-7 total score)

### Severity Classification

| Score | Severity | Clinical Action |
|-------|----------|----------------|
| 0-4 | Minimal | No intervention needed |
| 5-9 | Mild | Monitor; consider counseling; rescreen |
| 10-14 | Moderate | Treatment recommended: CBT and/or medication (SSRI/SNRI) |
| 15-21 | Severe | Active treatment: medication + therapy; consider psychiatry referral |

## AUDIT-C (Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test - Consumption)

### Questions

1. How often do you have a drink containing alcohol? (0=never, 1=monthly or less, 2=2-4 times/month, 3=2-3 times/week, 4=4+ times/week)
2. How many drinks containing alcohol do you have on a typical day when you are drinking? (0=1-2, 1=3-4, 2=5-6, 3=7-9, 4=10+)
3. How often do you have 6 or more drinks on one occasion? (0=never, 1=less than monthly, 2=monthly, 3=weekly, 4=daily or almost daily)

LOINC: 75626-2 (AUDIT-C total score)

### Screening Thresholds

| Population | Positive Screen | Action |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Men | >= 4 | Brief intervention, consider full AUDIT |
| Women | >= 3 | Brief intervention, consider full AUDIT |

### Full AUDIT Score Interpretation (10 questions)
| Score | Risk | Action |
|-------|------|--------|
| 0-7 | Low risk | Education |
| 8-15 | Hazardous | Brief intervention |
| 16-19 | Harmful | Brief intervention + continued monitoring |
| 20-40 | Possible dependence | Referral for diagnostic evaluation and treatment |

## Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS) -- Triage Version

### Screening Questions
1. Have you wished you were dead or wished you could go to sleep and not wake up? (passive ideation)
2. Have you had any actual thoughts of killing yourself? (active ideation)
3. Have you been thinking about how you might do this? (method)
4. Have you had these thoughts and had some intention of acting on them? (intent)
5. Have you started to work out or worked out the details of how to kill yourself? (plan)
6. Have you done anything, started to do anything, or prepared to do anything to end your life? (behavior)

LOINC: 93373-2

### Risk Triage
| Response | Risk Level | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| All "No" | Low | Standard care |
| "Yes" to Q1 only | Low-Moderate | Safety planning, follow-up |
| "Yes" to Q2 | Moderate | Safety planning, enhanced monitoring, mental health referral |
| "Yes" to Q3-5 | High | Psychiatric emergency evaluation |
| "Yes" to Q6 | Imminent | Immediate psychiatric emergency, do not leave alone |

## Common Psychotropic Medication Classes

| Class | Examples | Primary Indication |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| SSRIs | Sertraline, escitalopram, fluoxetine, paroxetine, citalopram | Depression, anxiety |
| SNRIs | Venlafaxine, duloxetine, desvenlafaxine | Depression, anxiety, pain |
| Bupropion | Bupropion XL/SR | Depression (avoid in seizure/eating disorders) |
| Mirtazapine | Mirtazapine | Depression (especially with insomnia/poor appetite) |
| Buspirone | Buspirone | Anxiety (non-addictive) |
| Benzodiazepines | Alprazolam, lorazepam, clonazepam | Anxiety (short-term only; dependency risk) |
| Mood stabilizers | Lithium, valproate, lamotrigine | Bipolar disorder |
| Atypical antipsychotics | Quetiapine, aripiprazole, olanzapine | Augmentation for depression, bipolar, psychosis |
