# WHO and CDC Growth Chart Percentile Reference

## Chart Selection

| Age | Chart | Basis |
|-----|-------|-------|
| 0-24 months | WHO Growth Standards | International, breastfed infants |
| 2-20 years | CDC Growth Charts | US reference population |

## Key Percentile Thresholds

### Weight-for-Age
| Percentile | Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------|
| < 3rd | Underweight -- evaluate for failure to thrive |
| 3rd-15th | Low-normal -- monitor closely |
| 15th-85th | Normal range |
| 85th-95th | Overweight risk |
| > 95th | Overweight |

### Length/Height-for-Age
| Percentile | Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------|
| < 3rd | Short stature -- evaluate (endocrine, constitutional, familial, nutritional) |
| 3rd-15th | Low-normal |
| 15th-85th | Normal range |
| > 97th | Tall stature -- usually constitutional; evaluate if concerning |

### BMI-for-Age (2-20 years)
| Percentile | Category |
|-----------|----------|
| < 5th | Underweight |
| 5th-84th | Normal/Healthy weight |
| 85th-94th | Overweight |
| >= 95th | Obese |
| >= 120% of 95th | Severe obesity |

### Head Circumference-for-Age (0-36 months)
| Percentile | Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------|
| < 3rd | Microcephaly -- evaluate (genetic, TORCH, syndromic) |
| 3rd-97th | Normal range |
| > 97th | Macrocephaly -- evaluate (familial, hydrocephalus, storage disease) |

## Growth Velocity Norms

### Expected Weight Gain
| Age | Expected Gain |
|-----|--------------|
| 0-3 months | 25-30 g/day (~1 oz/day) |
| 3-6 months | 15-20 g/day |
| 6-12 months | 10-13 g/day |
| 1-2 years | 200-250 g/month |
| 2-5 years | 150-200 g/month |
| Birth weight doubles | ~4-5 months |
| Birth weight triples | ~12 months |

### Expected Height Velocity
| Age | Expected Growth |
|-----|----------------|
| 0-12 months | ~25 cm/year |
| 1-2 years | ~12 cm/year |
| 2-4 years | ~7-8 cm/year |
| 4 years - puberty | ~5-6 cm/year |
| Puberty (girls) | ~8-12 cm/year (peak) |
| Puberty (boys) | ~10-14 cm/year (peak) |

## Growth Faltering Definition

Growth faltering (previously "failure to thrive"):
- Weight-for-age < 3rd percentile
- Weight-for-length < 3rd percentile
- Downward crossing of >= 2 major percentile lines on weight-for-age
- Weight velocity < 5th percentile for age

### Evaluation Checklist for Growth Faltering
1. Nutritional assessment (intake, feeding practices, food access)
2. GI evaluation (malabsorption: celiac, CF, IBD)
3. Endocrine evaluation (thyroid, growth hormone)
4. Chronic disease screening (renal, cardiac, pulmonary)
5. Psychosocial assessment (neglect, food insecurity)

## Premature Infant Corrected Age Calculation

Use corrected gestational age until 24 months for growth plotting:
- Corrected age = Chronological age - (40 weeks - gestational age at birth)
- Example: 12-month-old born at 32 weeks: corrected age = 12 - 8 = 4 months
