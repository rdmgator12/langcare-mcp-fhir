# AGS Beers Criteria 2023 Reference

## Table 2: Medications to Avoid in Older Adults (Regardless of Diagnosis)

### Anticholinergics
| Medication Class | Examples | Rationale | Alternative |
|-----------------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| First-gen antihistamines | Diphenhydramine, chlorpheniramine, hydroxyzine, promethazine | Highly anticholinergic; confusion, urinary retention, constipation, dry mouth; clearance reduced with age | Loratadine, cetirizine, fexofenadine |
| Antispasmodics | Dicyclomine, hyoscyamine, propantheline | Highly anticholinergic; uncertain effectiveness | Non-pharmacologic bowel management |
| Antiparkinsonians | Benztropine, trihexyphenidyl | Anticholinergic; not recommended to prevent EPS from antipsychotics | Use lowest antipsychotic dose |

### Central Nervous System
| Medication Class | Examples | Rationale | Alternative |
|-----------------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Benzodiazepines | Alprazolam, diazepam, lorazepam, clonazepam, temazepam | Falls, fractures, cognitive impairment, delirium; increased sensitivity | Trazodone (sleep), buspirone (anxiety), SSRI (anxiety) |
| Non-benzo hypnotics | Zolpidem, zaleplon, eszopiclone | Similar adverse effects to benzodiazepines; minimal efficacy improvement over placebo | CBT-I, melatonin, trazodone |
| Barbiturates | Phenobarbital (except seizure control), butalbital | High rate of dependence, tolerance; overdose risk | Triptans, NSAIDs (for headache) |
| Antipsychotics (for insomnia/agitation without psychosis) | All antipsychotics | Increased stroke risk, mortality in dementia; avoid for behavioral symptoms of dementia unless non-pharm failed | Non-pharmacologic approaches |
| Meprobamate | Meprobamate | Highly addictive, sedating | SSRI, buspirone |

### Cardiovascular
| Medication | Rationale | Alternative |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Peripheral alpha-1 blockers (doxazosin, prazosin, terazosin) for HTN | Orthostatic hypotension risk; not recommended as routine antihypertensive | Other antihypertensives; acceptable for BPH |
| Central alpha-agonists (clonidine, guanfacine, methyldopa) | CNS adverse effects; bradycardia, orthostatic hypotension | Other antihypertensives |
| Nifedipine (immediate-release) | Hypotension, myocardial ischemia risk | Extended-release CCBs |
| Digoxin >0.125 mg/day | Toxic levels with decreased renal clearance | Keep dose <= 0.125 mg/day |
| Amiodarone as first-line for AFib | Thyroid, pulmonary, QT toxicity | Rate control: metoprolol, diltiazem |

### Endocrine
| Medication | Rationale | Alternative |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Sulfonylureas (long-acting): glyburide, chlorpropamide | Prolonged hypoglycemia; glyburide higher risk than others | Glipizide (short-acting), metformin, SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA |
| Sliding-scale insulin (sole therapy) | Higher risk of hypoglycemia without improved control | Basal insulin or basal-bolus regimen |
| Testosterone (low T without clinical syndrome) | Cardiac risks; only use for confirmed hypogonadism | Evaluate underlying cause |

### Pain
| Medication | Rationale | Alternative |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| NSAIDs (chronic use) | GI bleeding, renal injury, CV risk; increased with age | Acetaminophen, topical NSAIDs, duloxetine |
| Indomethacin | Highest GI risk of all NSAIDs; CNS effects | Other analgesics |
| Skeletal muscle relaxants | Anticholinergic effects, sedation, fracture risk | Physical therapy, topical agents |
| Meperidine | Neurotoxic metabolite (normeperidine); seizure risk | Other opioids if needed |

## Table 3: Medications to Avoid with Specific Conditions

| Condition | Medications to Avoid | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------|
| Heart failure | NSAIDs, COX-2 inhibitors, diltiazem, verapamil, pioglitazone, cilostazol, dronedarone | Fluid retention, exacerbation |
| Syncope | AChEIs, alpha-blockers, TCAs, antipsychotics | Orthostatic hypotension |
| Delirium | Anticholinergics, benzodiazepines, corticosteroids, H2 blockers, meperidine, Z-drugs | Worsen delirium |
| Dementia | Anticholinergics, benzodiazepines, H2 blockers, antipsychotics (chronic) | Cognitive worsening |
| Falls/fractures | Anticonvulsants, antipsychotics, benzodiazepines, Z-drugs, opioids, SSRIs, SNRIs, TCAs | Increase fall risk |
| Parkinson disease | All antipsychotics (except quetiapine, pimavanserin), metoclopramide, prochlorperazine | Dopamine antagonism |
| CKD (Stage IV+) | NSAIDs, metformin (if eGFR <30) | Nephrotoxicity |
| Urinary incontinence | Alpha-blockers (in women), estrogen (oral/transdermal) | Worsen incontinence |
| BPH/lower urinary tract symptoms | Strongly anticholinergic drugs, inhaled anticholinergics | Urinary retention |
| Constipation (chronic) | Oral antimuscarinics for urinary incontinence, first-gen antihistamines, anticholinergics | Worsen constipation |
| Seizure disorder | Bupropion, chlorpromazine, clozapine, tramadol | Lower seizure threshold |
| Insomnia | Oral decongestants, stimulants, theophylline, caffeine | Worsen insomnia |
| Gastroparesis | Anticholinergics, opioids, GLP-1 RA | Worsen motility |

## Anticholinergic Burden Score (ACB)

| Score | Medications |
|-------|------------|
| 3 (definite) | Amitriptyline, atropine, benztropine, chlorpromazine, clomipramine, clozapine, darifenacin, dicyclomine, diphenhydramine, doxepin, flavoxate, hydroxyzine, hyoscyamine, imipramine, meclizine, nortriptyline, oxybutynin, paroxetine, perphenazine, promethazine, scopolamine, solifenacin, thioridazine, tolterodine, trifluoperazine, trihexyphenidyl, trimipramine |
| 2 (probable) | Amantadine, baclofen, carbamazepine, cetirizine, cimetidine, clozapine, cyclobenzaprine, cyproheptadine, loperamide, loratadine, nifedipine, olanzapine, ranitidine, tizanidine |
| 1 (possible) | Alprazolam, aripiprazole, atenolol, bupropion, captopril, chlorthalidone, citalopram, codeine, colchicine, diazepam, digoxin, dipyridamole, fentanyl, furosemide, haloperidol, hydralazine, hydrocortisone, isosorbide, metoprolol, morphine, prednisone, quetiapine, risperidone, theophylline, tramadol, trazodone, venlafaxine, warfarin |

Total ACB >= 3: associated with increased cognitive decline, delirium risk, mortality.
