## Methadone Conversion (Variable -- Dose-Dependent)

Methadone has a non-linear conversion that increases with total daily dose:

| Oral Morphine Equivalent (mg/day) | Methadone MME Factor |
|-----------------------------------|---------------------|
| 1-20 mg/day methadone | 4.0 |
| 21-40 mg/day methadone | 8.0 |
| 41-60 mg/day methadone | 10.0 |
| >60 mg/day methadone | 12.0 |

**Warning**: Methadone conversions are approximate. Methadone has unique pharmacokinetics (long half-life 8-59 hours, QT prolongation risk). Use extreme caution.

## Buprenorphine Notes

- Buprenorphine for pain (Butrans patch, Belbuca buccal): included in MME calculation
  - Transdermal (mcg/hr x 24): use factor of 12.6 for daily MME
  - Buccal film: use factor of 4.4 for daily MME
- Buprenorphine for OUD (Suboxone, Sublocade): NOT included in MME calculations per CDC guidance
- Buprenorphine has a ceiling effect on respiratory depression

## CDC 2022 Risk Thresholds

| Total Daily MME | Risk Level | Clinical Action |
|----------------|------------|-----------------|
| < 20 | Lower risk | Standard monitoring; reassess in 1-4 weeks initially, then every 3 months |
| 20-49 | Moderate risk | Reassess benefits vs risks; non-opioid alternatives; functional goals |
| 50-89 | CAUTION | Increased overdose risk; prescribe naloxone; consider specialist referral |
| >= 90 | HIGH RISK | Avoid prescribing at this level; if already here, discuss tapering; naloxone mandatory |
