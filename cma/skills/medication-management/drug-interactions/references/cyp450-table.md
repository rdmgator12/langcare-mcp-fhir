# CYP450 Metabolism Reference Table

## Major CYP450 Enzymes and Clinical Substrates

### CYP3A4 (metabolizes ~50% of drugs)

**Strong Inhibitors** (avoid combination or reduce substrate dose >80%):
- Ketoconazole, itraconazole, voriconazole, posaconazole
- Clarithromycin, telithromycin
- Ritonavir, cobicistat, nelfinavir
- Grapefruit juice (variable)

**Moderate Inhibitors** (may need substrate dose reduction ~50%):
- Erythromycin, fluconazole, diltiazem, verapamil
- Aprepitant, crizotinib, imatinib

**Strong Inducers** (may need substrate dose increase or avoid):
- Rifampin, rifabutin
- Phenytoin, carbamazepine, phenobarbital
- St. John's wort

**Key Substrates** (levels affected by above):
- Statins: atorvastatin, lovastatin, simvastatin (NOT pravastatin, rosuvastatin)
- Benzodiazepines: midazolam, triazolam, alprazolam
- Calcium channel blockers: amlodipine, felodipine, nifedipine
- Immunosuppressants: cyclosporine, tacrolimus, sirolimus
- Anticoagulants: apixaban, rivaroxaban
- Opioids: fentanyl, oxycodone, methadone
- Others: sildenafil, colchicine, ticagrelor

### CYP2D6 (metabolizes ~25% of drugs)

**Strong Inhibitors**:
- Fluoxetine, paroxetine
- Bupropion
- Quinidine
- Terbinafine

**Moderate Inhibitors**:
- Duloxetine, sertraline (weak-moderate)
- Diphenhydramine
- Amiodarone

**Key Substrates**:
- Opioids: codeine (prodrug), tramadol (prodrug), hydrocodone
- Beta-blockers: metoprolol, carvedilol, propranolol
- Antidepressants: venlafaxine, desipramine, nortriptyline
- Antipsychotics: haloperidol, risperidone, aripiprazole
- Tamoxifen (prodrug -- inhibition reduces efficacy)
- Atomoxetine, dextromethorphan

### CYP2C19

**Strong Inhibitors**:
- Fluoxetine, fluvoxamine
- Omeprazole, esomeprazole (moderate)
- Ticlopidine

**Key Substrates**:
- Clopidogrel (prodrug -- inhibition reduces activation)
- PPIs: omeprazole, lansoprazole, pantoprazole
- Antiepileptics: phenytoin, diazepam
- Voriconazole

### CYP2C9

**Strong Inhibitors**:
- Fluconazole, miconazole
- Amiodarone
- Metronidazole (moderate)

**Key Substrates**:
- Warfarin (S-warfarin)
- NSAIDs: celecoxib, ibuprofen, naproxen
- Sulfonylureas: glipizide, glyburide
- Phenytoin, losartan

### CYP1A2

**Strong Inhibitors**:
- Fluvoxamine, ciprofloxacin
- Cimetidine (moderate)

**Strong Inducers**:
- Smoking (tobacco), charbroiled foods
- Rifampin, omeprazole (mild)

**Key Substrates**:
- Theophylline, caffeine
- Clozapine, olanzapine
- Tizanidine (contraindicated with ciprofloxacin/fluvoxamine)
- Warfarin (R-warfarin)

## Common Pharmacodynamic Interactions

### QT Prolongation Risk (additive)
Medications with QT risk: amiodarone, sotalol, dofetilide, fluoroquinolones, azithromycin, ondansetron, haloperidol, methadone, citalopram/escitalopram (doses >40/20mg), hydroxychloroquine

### Serotonin Syndrome Risk
Serotonergic drugs: SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, triptans, tramadol, fentanyl, linezolid, methylene blue, St. John's wort, dextromethorphan, lithium

### Bleeding Risk (additive)
Anticoagulants + antiplatelets + NSAIDs + SSRIs/SNRIs + fish oil + garlic supplements

### CNS Depression (additive)
Opioids + benzodiazepines + gabapentinoids + muscle relaxants + Z-drugs + alcohol + antihistamines

### Anticholinergic Burden (additive)
First-gen antihistamines + TCAs + antipsychotics + bladder antimuscarinics + scopolamine + atropine
